以神之名，行男权之刃In the Name of God, By the Blade of Patriarchy

国际 直接层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-23 § 链接
宗教教义的极端解读，是男性中心叙事最廉价且高效的暴力武器。
Extreme religious interpretation is the cheapest and most efficient weapon of the masculine-centric narrative.

萨尔曼·鲁什迪在法庭上摘下眼镜，向陪审团展示他失明的右眼。这只眼睛的缺失，是直接暴力 (direct violence) 最直观的物证。但真正值得拆解的是这把刀背后的逻辑：一个 28 岁的年轻人，在 1989 年的一道 fatwa（宗教法令）和 2006 年的煽动下，决定在 2022 年实施一场“圣战”。

这就是典型的表达武器化。宗教在此时不再是精神寄托，而是一个被垄断的认知入口。原教旨主义通过垄断对经文的解释权，将“亵渎”定义为不可原谅的罪行，从而为肉体消灭提供合法性。这种叙事将直接暴力包装成神圣的义务，让施暴者在潜意识中完成了从“谋杀犯”到“信仰捍卫者”的身份转换。这正是文化暴力 (cultural violence) 的核心功能：让血腥的屠杀看起来像是正义的执行。

更深层的元暴力 (meta violence) 在于，这场跨越三十年的追杀，本质上是一场关于“谁有权定义真实”的权力战争。男性权力中心通过宗教指令，试图抹除一个不顺从的叙事者。在这种男性中心叙事中，对“神”的忠诚被等同于对男性权力结构的绝对服从。任何挑战这一结构的表达，都被标记为必须被清除的异端。

法庭上的辩论在纠结于 Matar 袭击时的具体“心态”和“意图”，但这在结构性分析面前毫无意义。当一个社会允许将“杀人”与“圣战”在认知层面挂钩时，个体的意图只是这台暴力机器上的一个零件。鲁什迪说“我认为在这里受审的是另一个人”，这不仅是文学家的风骨，更是对元暴力的精准反击：他拒绝让自己的作品成为这场暴力博弈的辩护词。

Salman Rushdie removed his glasses in court to show the jury his sightless right eye. This loss is the most visceral evidence of direct violence. However, the logic behind the blade requires a deeper autopsy: a 28-year-old man, driven by a 1989 fatwa and 2006 incitements, decided to wage a "jihad" in 2022.

This is the weaponisation of expression in its purest form. Religion here is no longer a spiritual sanctuary but a monopolised cognitive entry point. Fundamentalism, by monopolising the interpretation of scripture, defines "blasphemy" as an unforgivable crime, thereby legitimising physical liquidation. This narrative transforms a murderer into a "defender of faith" in the perpetrator's mind. This is the precise function of cultural violence: making bloodlust appear as divine justice.

The underlying meta violence is a war over who owns the right to define reality. The masculine-centric narrative, using religious edicts, attempts to erase a non-compliant narrator. In this structure, loyalty to "God" is a proxy for absolute submission to the patriarchal power hierarchy. Any expression that challenges this structure is branded as a heresy that must be purged.

The legal battle focuses on Matar's specific "intent" and "mindset," but such details are irrelevant in a structural analysis. When a society allows the conceptual merging of "murder" and "holy war," individual intent becomes merely a cog in the violence machine. Rushdie's remark that "someone else is on trial" is not just literary poise, but a precise strike against meta violence: he refuses to let his work serve as a justification for this game of brutality.
