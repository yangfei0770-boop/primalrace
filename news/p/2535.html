Farage 的“受害者”剧本与权力共谋的遮羞布Farage's 'Victim' Script and the Shroud of Power Complicity

国际 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-17 § 链接
将财务丑闻定义为“政治迫害”，是权力持有者最廉价的武器化叙事。
Defining financial scandals as 'political persecution' is the cheapest weaponized narrative for power holders.

Nigel Farage 把对 500 万英镑秘密捐款的质疑称为“协同围攻” (coordinated pile-on)，并声称自己被“非人化” (dehumanised)。这套话术极其熟练：他试图通过将财务透明度问题转化为一种“身份政治”的受害者叙事，来夺取解释权。当一个掌握巨额加密货币资金、在权力中心博弈的政客开始表演“被欺凌的弱者”时，这不仅是 scam，更是一次典型的表达武器化。

Farage 在 CPAC GB 这种硬右翼集会上煽动情绪，利用支持者的共情来掩盖结构性的违规。他把对法律和标准的质疑定义为“建制派的打压”，从而在支持者心中建立一套只有他与“真实民众”对立于“虚伪精英”的二元叙事。这种叙事入口的本质是：只要我定义自己为受害者，那么任何事实的揭露都变成了对我的“暴力”。

更深层的共谋在于，这类右翼政治生态通过制造“外部敌人”来维持内部团结。无论是加密货币富豪的秘密注资，还是与诈骗犯的私交，这些财务黑洞在“反建制”的旗号下被合法化。他们共谋地定义一套新的“真实”：规则是建制派用来限制真诚者的工具，而违规则是为了打破枷锁的必要代价。

Farage 所谓的“赌一把” (taken a punt)，赌的不是选票，而是这套受害者剧本在认知入口上的统治力。他并不在乎财务是否清白，他在乎的是能否通过这次“围攻”，将自己进一步神化为体制的祭品。在这种元暴力的逻辑下，事实不再重要，只有谁能把“被攻击”演得更像一种勋章。

Nigel Farage labels questions about his £5m secret donations as a 'coordinated pile-on' and claims he has been 'dehumanised.' This playbook is textbook: he attempts to transform a matter of financial transparency into a victim narrative of identity politics to seize the power of interpretation. When a politician fueled by crypto-billions performs the role of the 'bullied underdog,' it is not just a scam; it is a classic weaponization of expression.

By inciting emotions at an event like CPAC GB, Farage uses the empathy of his base to mask structural violations. He defines the scrutiny of legal standards as 'establishment suppression,' constructing a binary narrative where only he and the 'real people' stand against a 'hypocritical uniparty.' The essence of this cognitive entry point is simple: if I define myself as the victim, any revelation of fact becomes an act of 'violence' against me.

The deeper complicity lies in how this right-wing ecosystem maintains internal solidarity by manufacturing external enemies. Secret infusions from crypto-wealth and friendships with convicted fraudsters are legitimized under the banner of 'anti-establishmentism.' They collude to define a new 'reality': rules are merely tools for the elite to restrict the sincere, and violations are the necessary price for breaking chains.

Farage's claim that he has 'taken a punt' is not a gamble on votes, but on the dominance of this victim script. He doesn't care about financial purity; he cares about whether this 'pile-on' can further sanctify him as a martyr of the system. Under this logic of meta-violence, facts are irrelevant; only the ability to perform 'persecution' as a badge of honor matters.
