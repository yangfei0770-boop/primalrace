霍尔木兹海峡的火光与男性权力的自嗨Fire in the Strait and the Narcissism of Masculine Power

国际 直接层 · 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-21 § 链接
战争是男性中心叙事最极端的表达，将身体降格为宏大叙事的燃料。
War is the most extreme expression of masculine-centric narrative, reducing bodies to fuel for grand narratives.

看这篇新闻，你会发现一个极其荒诞的循环：美国在轰炸，伊朗在袭击，胡塞武装在封锁。所有的动作词都是“degrade”（削弱）、“compelled”（强迫）、“pay many times over”（付出多倍代价）。这不仅是地缘政治的博弈，更是一场典型的 masculine-centric narrative 的暴力表演。在这种叙事里，世界被简化为几个强力男性（Trump、伊朗总统、革命卫队）在地图上划线，而具体的生命——无论是被强迫弃船的船员，还是在空袭中死亡的士兵——都被降格为可消耗的工具，成了这场存在性战争中的 a set of data。

最讽刺的是，这种暴力被包装成“保护国际法”或“反抗不公正封锁”的武器化叙事。所谓的“必要措施”和“正义回应”，本质上是元暴力（meta violence）的延伸：谁掌握了定义“正义”的解释权，谁就能合法地在结构层实施暴力。而在这个闭环里，女性和非战斗员的生存状态完全被抹除。在这个被男性统治的战争剧本中，女性要么是需要被“解放”的背景板，要么是作为战争代价被无视的附庸。

别被油价的波动或外交斡旋的戏码骗了。当权力者在讨论如何“degrade”对方的能力时，他们共谋的是一套关于“强权即真理”的旧逻辑。这种共谋不仅发生在美伊之间，也发生在所有认同这种“强人政治”的观众心中。这场战争没有 winner，只有在不同层级的 violence triangle 中被不断榨干的肉体。

Scanning this news, you see a surreal loop: the US is bombing, Iran is attacking, and the Houthis are blockading. The vocabulary is dominated by terms like “degrade,” “compelled,” and “pay many times over.” This is not just geopolitical gambling; it is a textbook performance of masculine-centric narrative. In this script, the world is reduced to a few powerful men—Trump, the Iranian president, the IRGC—drawing lines on a map. Actual lives, from the crew forced to abandon their ship to the soldiers killed in airstrikes, are downgraded into disposable tools, merely a set of data in this existential war.

The irony lies in how this violence is weaponized through narratives of “protecting international law” or “responding to unjust blockades.” These “necessary measures” and “just responses” are simply extensions of meta-violence: whoever monopolizes the interpretation of “justice” gains the right to implement structural violence. In this closed loop, the existence of women and non-combatants is completely erased. In this male-dominated war script, women are either background scenery waiting to be “liberated” or appendages ignored as collateral damage.

Don’t be fooled by fluctuating oil prices or the theater of diplomatic mediation. When power-holders discuss how to “degrade” an opponent's capability, they are in complicity with an archaic logic where “might makes right.” This complicity exists not only between the US and Iran but among all spectators who validate this “strongman politics.” There are no winners here—only bodies being systematically drained across the layers of the violence triangle.
