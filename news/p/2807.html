玻璃笼子里的“恐怖分子”与国家机器的共谋Glass Cages and the Complicity of State Machinery

国际 直接层 · 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-24 § 链接
用“恐怖主义”定义财产损失，是结构暴力合法化的标准叙事。
Defining property damage as 'terrorism' is the standard narrative for legitimizing structural violence.

把五个破坏窗户和设备的活动家关进玻璃笼子，每天禁闭23小时，这在德国法律的叙事里叫“防范社会威胁”。但如果你把尺子换成加尔通的暴力三角，这就是典型的 structural violence：通过重新定义“犯罪”的性质，将对私有财产的破坏等同于对人类安全的威胁，从而为直接的身体禁锢（direct violence）提供文化合法性。

这里的关键在于“解释权”的武器化。德国和英国的司法系统在玩同一个 scam：将 Palestine Action 的活动定义为具有“恐怖主义关联”。一旦这个标签贴上，法律就不再是保护权利的工具，而变成了清除异己的武器。当一个23岁的人因为试图中断武器供应而被剥夺与律师自由沟通的权利时，这个“玻璃墙”不仅是物理的，更是认知上的——它在告诉世界，只要你挑战了国家与军工复合体的利益共谋，你就不再被视为享有完整人权的“公民”，而是一个需要被隔离的“客体”。

爱尔兰政客的介入是一次微小的 meta 层博弈，试图通过外交压力将这个黑盒透明化。但我们要警惕这种“人道主义关注”的表演性。如果关注仅停留在“待遇是否太差”，而没有触及“为何破坏军工设备被定义为恐怖主义”这一元暴力逻辑，那么这种关注不过是在既定规则内的一次温情修补。真正的胜利不应该是让囚室大一点，而是让那个玻璃笼子彻底粉碎。

Locking five activists in glass cages and confining them for 23 hours a day is framed as 'preventing social threats' in the German legal narrative. But using Galtung's Violence Triangle, this is textbook structural violence: by redefining the nature of the 'crime,' they equate property damage with threats to human safety, providing cultural legitimacy for direct physical confinement.

The crux here is the weaponisation of 'interpretative power.' The German and British judicial systems are running the same scam: labeling Palestine Action's activities as having a 'terrorist connection.' Once this tag is applied, the law ceases to be a tool for protecting rights and becomes a weapon for purging dissent. When a 23-year-old is denied free communication with lawyers for trying to interrupt arms deliveries, the 'glass wall' is not just physical—it is cognitive. It signals that anyone challenging the complicity between the state and the military-industrial complex is no longer a 'citizen' with full human rights, but an 'object' to be isolated.

The Irish politicians' intervention is a minor meta-level game, attempting to make this black box transparent through diplomatic pressure. However, we must be wary of the performative nature of 'humanitarian concern.' If the focus remains only on whether 'treatment is too harsh' without challenging the meta-violence—why destroying arms factory equipment is defined as terrorism—then this concern is merely a sentimental patch within existing rules. True victory is not a slightly larger cell, but the total shattering of the glass cage.
