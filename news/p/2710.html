被焚毁的物证与被抹除的结构暴力Burned Evidence and the Erased Structural Violence

哲学 结构层 · 文化层 The New York Times ↗ 2026-07-22 § 链接
悲剧的叙事重心在遗物，而暴力的核心在成本核算。
The narrative focuses on relics, while the violence lies in the cost-benefit analysis.

这篇报道在进行一种典型的文化层操弄：它用极具感染力的细节——半瓶牛奶、缝制的牛仔背心、闪光的钻戒——将一场结构性屠杀转化为个体哀悼的抒情诗。当读者的注意力被这些“遗物”占据时，这场火灾最核心的暴力逻辑被稀释了：168条人命的代价，竟然是由于承包商为了榨取最大利润而使用了劣质材料。这就是典型的 structural violence。在资本的成本核算表里，劣质材料带来的利润差额，被定价为168个人的生命。而这种定价权，始终掌握在那些不被报道、不出现在废墟中的 contractor 手中。

更令人不安的是叙事中的共谋痕迹。报道中出现了多位印尼籍家庭帮佣（domestic workers），她们在火灾中与雇主一同死去。在这些家庭的叙事里，她们被描述为“善良的”、“照顾周到的”，这种温情的个体评价掩盖了她们作为“原初种族”在结构中的极度脆弱。她们跨越国境，进入一个陌生的权力结构，在雇主的私人空间里提供低廉劳动力，而当结构性暴力爆发时，她们是第一批被献祭的。她们的死在叙事中成了雇主丧亲之痛的背景板，而非一个关于全球劳动力剥削和安全保障缺失的政治议题。

这种“以物证情”的报道方式，本质上是在制造一种伪共情。它让人们在感叹“人生无常”和“亲情可贵”的同时，潜意识里接受了这种死亡是某种“意外”或“不幸”，而非一种必然的暴力结果。如果 Potential（安全的居住环境）与 Actual（劣质材料的危楼）之间的差额就是暴力，那么这场火灾不是意外，而是一场蓄谋已久的、由贪婪驱动的结构性谋杀。不要被那些闪光的钻戒骗了，真正的暴力是那些被省掉的防火材料。

This report performs a classic cultural manipulation: it transforms a structural massacre into a lyrical poem of individual mourning using evocative details—half-finished milk, hand-sewn denim vests, sparkling rings. As the audience's attention is captured by these "relics," the core logic of the violence is diluted. The cost of 168 lives was simply the profit margin gained by unscrupulous contractors using shoddy materials. This is quintessential structural violence. In the cost-benefit analysis of capital, the price difference of materials was priced as 168 human lives. This pricing power remains in the hands of the contractors who are never depicted in the ruins.

Even more disturbing is the trace of complicity within the narrative. Several Indonesian domestic workers are mentioned, dying alongside their employers. In these family stories, they are described as "kind" or "caring." These warm, individual evaluations mask their extreme vulnerability as the Primal Race within a global structure. They crossed borders to provide cheap labor in private spaces, and when structural violence erupted, they were the first to be sacrificed. In the narrative, their deaths serve as a backdrop to the grief of their employers rather than a political issue of labor exploitation and systemic safety failure.

This "object-driven」 storytelling creates a pseudo-empathy. It leads people to accept these deaths as "tragedies" or "misfortune" while ignoring them as inevitable results of violence. If violence is the gap between Potential (safe housing) and Actual (shoddy ruins), then this fire was not an accident, but a calculated structural murder driven by greed. Do not be deceived by the sparkling diamonds; the real violence is the fireproofing materials that were cut from the budget.
