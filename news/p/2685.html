把气候危机翻译成关税单：特朗普及 MAGA 的认知入口操纵术Translating Climate Crisis into Tariff Sheets: Trump and MAGA's Manipulation of Cognitive Entry

国际 结构层 · 文化层 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-22 § 链接
用具体的“敌人”掩盖结构性的崩溃，是权力者最廉价的武器化叙事。
Replacing structural collapse with a concrete 'enemy' is the cheapest weaponized narrative for power.

特朗普及 MAGA 阵营对加拿大林火烟雾的反应，是一次教科书级别的“表达武器化”操作。面对全球气候危机这个复杂的 Structural Violence，他们迅速将其通过认知入口（Cognitive Entry）简化为一场关于“入侵”和“污染”的敌我博弈。烟雾这种不承认边境的物理事实，在他们的叙事中被翻译成了可以被制裁、被课税的政治筹码。

这种操作的本质是夺取解释权。当 Mark Carney 试图将讨论拉回“气候危机”这一人类共同责任的 Meta 层级时，Trump 迅速用“赔偿”和“关税”将其拽回一个简单的、基于强弱关系的交易模型中。在这种模型里，事实不重要，重要的是谁在定义“不可接受”。

最荒诞的共谋在于，Fox News 等媒体通过密集输出对加拿大的攻击，成功地在受众大脑中建立了一套掩体：只要我们在愤怒地指责邻居，我们就可以心安理得地忽略那个正在把世界点燃的 root cause。这是一种典型的文化暴力（Cultural Violence），它让结构性的生态崩溃看起来像是一次简单的外交摩擦，从而使权力者能够继续在世界燃烧时，心安理得地玩弄他们的关税游戏。

Trump and the MAGA camp's reaction to the Canadian wildfires is a textbook case of the Weaponisation of Expression. Facing the climate crisis—a complex form of Structural Violence—they swiftly simplified it through a Cognitive Entry into a binary game of 'invasion' and 'pollution.' The physical fact of smoke, which recognizes no borders, was translated in their narrative into political chips for sanctions and tariffs.

This is fundamentally about seizing the power of interpretation. While Mark Carney attempted to pivot the discussion toward the Meta-level of collective human responsibility for the climate crisis, Trump immediately dragged it back to a simple transaction model based on power dynamics. In this model, facts are irrelevant; what matters is who defines what is 'unacceptable.'

The most absurd part is the complicity of Fox News and similar media. By intensively directing anger toward Canada, they construct a narrative shelter: as long as we are outraged at our neighbor, we can comfortably ignore the root cause setting the world on fire. This is classic Cultural Violence, making structural ecological collapse look like a mere diplomatic spat, allowing those in power to continue fiddling with tariffs while the world burns.
