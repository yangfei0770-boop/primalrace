谁在为“激进”定价：Miatta的权力入场券Pricing the 'Radical': Miatta's Ticket to Power

国际 结构层 · 文化层 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-24 § 链接
所谓的“激进”在权力顶层是可定价的资产，而非颠覆的武器。
Being 'radical' at the top of the hierarchy is a priced asset, not a weapon of disruption.

一个从难民背景出发，在LSE拿PhD，最后进入内阁的“激进经济学家”，这在Westminster的叙事里是一个完美的 progressive 样板。但我们要看的是，这种“激进” (radical) 是在什么环境下被定义的？当她领导NEF设计“绿色新政”时，她是在定义一种可能性；而当她成为能源大臣，面对Jackdaw和Rosebank这两个具体的钻探项目时，她面对的是真正的 structural violence —— 那些被写死在法律程序里、由资本和能源巨头共谋的既定利益。

好玩的地方在于，媒体在讨论她是否会“坚持底线” (hold the line)。在权力博弈中，底线从来不是由个人的道德纯度决定的，而是由你所处的共谋场域决定的。她之前的辞职信写得非常 blunt，这种“不妥协”的表达，在进入内阁后，很可能成为她与党内温和派、工会以及能源巨头进行交易的筹码。她的“激进”标签，实际上成了她进入权力核心的最优解表达：一个能给政府带来“环保合法性”的门面。

如果她最终批准了钻探，那么这次任命就是一次典型的 weaponized narrative：用一个激进者的身份，去掩盖一个保守的决策。如果她真的砍掉了钻探，那才是真正的 a good_news，因为这意味着 Actual 终于向 Potential 走近了一步，削减了结构层面的暴力。但在目前的男性中心叙事中，一个女性在能源这种高度男性化的权力领域中，最容易被赋予的角色就是“良心”或“激进者”——这种定位本身就是一种软性的规训，将她的专业能力窄化为一种政治姿态。

A 'radical economist' from a refugee background with an LSE PhD ascending to the Cabinet is a perfect progressive trope for Westminster. But we must ask: in what context is this 'radicalism' defined? While leading the NEF, she defined possibilities; as Energy Secretary facing the Jackdaw and Rosebank fields, she faces actual structural violence — the vested interests of capital and energy giants hard-coded into legal procedures.

The irony lies in the discourse of 'holding the line.' In power games, the line is never determined by individual moral purity, but by the field of complicity one inhabits. Her previous 'blunt' resignation is a form of expression that, once inside the Cabinet, likely becomes a bargaining chip with moderates and unions. Her 'radical' label is, in fact, the optimal expression for her ascent: a facade providing 'environmental legitimacy' to the government.

If she eventually approves the drilling, this appointment is a classic case of weaponized narrative: using a radical identity to mask a conservative decision. Only if she scraps the drilling would this be a good_news, as it would mean the Actual is finally moving toward the Potential, reducing structural violence. However, in the masculine-centric narrative, a woman in the hyper-masculine energy sector is often cast as the 'conscience' or the 'zealot' — a subtle form of regulation that narrows her professional agency into a mere political posture.
