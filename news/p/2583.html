事实的“观点化”是元暴力的完美掩体The 'Opinionization' of Fact: A Perfect Shield for Meta-Violence

国际 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-20 § 链接
当事实被定义为观点，监管就成了对谎言的共谋。
When facts are redefined as opinions, regulation becomes complicity in lying.

房子在烧，车在炸，但主持人说“没有暴乱”。这不是在进行某种深刻的政治解读，而是在直接抹除物理现实。最荒诞的不是这种睁眼说瞎话的表达，而是监管机构 Ofcom 的逻辑：因为节目里有个嘉宾反驳了她，所以这就算作是“观点的表达”，不需要调查。

这是一个典型的 weaponized 叙事入口。通过将“房子是否被烧毁”这种 settled facts 降格为“个人观点”，GB News 成功地在认知入口处制造了一个真空地带。在这种逻辑下，事实不再是基准，而成了可以被随意揉捏的素材。只要在演播室里安排一个“反方”扮演挑战者，谎言就获得了“多样性”的合法外衣。

Ofcom 的不作为不是监管失职，而是一种 structural violence 的共谋。它向所有媒体传递了一个信号：只要你给谎言配一个反对者，你就可以在公共空间肆意定义现实。这种对解释权的垄断，正是元暴力 (meta violence) 的运作方式——它不直接通过禁令封杀真相，而是通过降低事实的权重，让真相在“观点多样性”的噪音中被稀释，直到大众不再关心什么是事实。

当监管者宣布“事实不重要，只要有争论就行”时，它实际上是在为未来的所有社会动荡预埋认知地雷。如果暴乱可以被定义为“不那么戏剧化”，那么接下来的任何权力扩张或暴力升级，都可以通过同样的逻辑被定义为“必要的秩序”。

Houses were burning, cars were exploding, yet the presenter insisted there were 'no riots.' This isn't a nuanced political interpretation; it is the direct erasure of physical reality. The most absurd part isn't the blatant lie, but Ofcom's logic: because a guest challenged her, this is categorized as an 'expression of personal view' and requires no investigation.

This is a classic weaponization of the cognitive entry point. By downgrading settled facts—like whether a house burned down—into 'opinions,' GB News successfully creates a vacuum of truth. In this framework, facts are no longer the benchmark but raw material to be manipulated. As long as a 'challenger' is placed in the studio, the lie is granted the legitimacy of 'diversity.'

Ofcom's inaction is not mere negligence; it is a form of complicity in structural violence. It signals to all broadcasters that as long as you provide a dissenting voice, you can redefine reality at will. This monopoly over the power of interpretation is exactly how meta-violence operates—not by banning truth, but by diluting it within the noise of 'diverse opinions' until the public stops caring about what is actually true.

When a regulator declares that 'facts don't matter as long as there is a debate,' it plants cognitive landmines for future unrest. If a riot can be defined as 'not that dramatic,' then any subsequent expansion of power or escalation of violence can be similarly defined as 'necessary order.'
