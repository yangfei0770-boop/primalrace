地标重启的浪漫叙事与资本的认知入口The Romantic Narrative of Landmark Revival and Capital's Cognitive Entry

哲学 文化层 · 结构层 The New York Times ↗ 2026-07-22 § 链接
所谓的“行业复苏”不过是资本在完成新一轮定价权后的认知收割。
The so-called "industry recovery" is merely a cognitive harvest after capital completes a new round of pricing power.

Sony 宣布重启 Cinerama Dome，把一个被封死六年的建筑包装成好莱坞“复苏”的图腾。Tom Rothman 那句 “Hot damn!” 充满了一种典型的男性中心主义式的情绪煽动——将商业地产的租赁协议升华为对电影艺术的“灵魂信仰”。这种叙事极其熟练地将认知入口引导向“情怀”与“地标”，试图让大众忘记在 streaming 浪潮和疫情期间，电影工业是如何通过削减成本、压低创作者收益来完成结构性止损的。

这次重启的本质不是艺术的回归，而是一次精准的博弈。Sony 购买 Alamo Drafthouse 并在 Dome 引入 premium-format，实际上是在重新定义“电影体验”的定价权。当大众被告知“年轻观众需要沉浸式体验”时，资本在做的是将电影院从一个公共文化空间，转化为一个高客单价的、由激光投影和精选餐饮支撑的消费陷阱。这是一种典型的武器化表达：用“地标重启”的浪漫外壳，掩盖其通过垄断优质放映资源来榨取超额利润的商业逻辑。

至于所谓的 Box Office 增长 10%，在结构性暴力面前只是一个统计学上的噪音。电影工业的权力重心并未下移，解释权依然掌握在像 Sony 这样能够通过跨境资本运作、吞并发行链条的巨头手中。Cinerama Dome 再次亮灯，不代表电影院这个物种地狱般地活了过来，而代表资本终于找到了一个能让它在 2028 年再次通过“仪式感”收割观众的新入口。

Sony's announcement to revive the Cinerama Dome packages a six-year shuttered building as a totem of Hollywood's "recovery." Tom Rothman's "Hot damn!" is a classic example of masculine-centric emotional manipulation—elevating a commercial lease agreement into a "soulful belief" in cinematic art. This narrative skillfully directs the cognitive entry toward "nostalgia" and "landmarks," attempting to make the public forget how the film industry performed structural cost-cutting and suppressed creator earnings during the streaming surge and the pandemic.

The essence of this revival is not an artistic return, but a precise game of existence. By acquiring Alamo Drafthouse and introducing premium-formats to the Dome, Sony is effectively redefining the pricing power of the "movie-going experience." While the public is told that "younger audiences demand immersive experiences," capital is transforming the cinema from a public cultural space into a high-ticket consumption trap supported by laser projection and curated dining. This is a textbook weaponization of expression: using the romantic shell of a "landmark revival" to mask a commercial logic of extracting excess profits through the monopoly of premium exhibition resources.

As for the 10% growth in box office sales, it is mere statistical noise in the face of structural violence. The center of gravity in the film industry has not shifted; the power of interpretation remains with giants like Sony, who can manipulate cross-border capital and absorb distribution chains. The relighting of the Cinerama Dome does not mean the cinema as a species has miraculously survived; it means capital has finally found a new entry point to harvest audiences through "ritualism" in 2028.
