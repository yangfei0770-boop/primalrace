禁用水管是典型的结构性暴力PRHosepipe Bans as a PR Mask for Structural Violence

国际 结构层 · 文化层 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-21 § 链接
将系统性的资源管理失败，通过个体禁令转化为公民的道德义务。
Converting systemic resource failure into a moral obligation for individuals through administrative bans.

Thames Water 宣布禁用水管 (hosepipe ban)，在叙事上被包装成为了“保护所有人”的必要之举。但这实际上是一场典型的 structural violence。水公司将资源枯竭的责任，通过一个简单的行政禁令，从管理层的战略失效转移到了 2300 万个体的日常行为上。

所谓的“需求增加 10%”是一个精心设计的认知入口。水公司通过将需求量比作“增加了一个曼彻斯特市”来制造危机感，却绝口不提基础设施的陈旧、管网渗漏以及资本在水资源管理上的长期缺位。当系统 Potential（本应具备的抗旱能力）与 Actual（实际供应能力）之间出现巨大差额时，水公司选择的不是修复结构，而是通过 cultural violence——即通过“环保”和“共担”的叙事，让被剥夺用水权的公民在潜意识中认同这种禁令的“正当性”。

这不仅是环境危机，更是一场共谋。政府、水公司与气象部门共同构建了一个“气候极端化”的不可抗力叙事，将结构性暴力掩盖在自然灾害的阴影下。在这种 masculine-centric 的管理逻辑中，解决问题的方式永远是下达指令、划定禁区、实施限制，而不是反思权力与资源分配的原始缺陷。禁用水管不是在救火，而是在通过表演性让步，掩盖一个正在崩塌的资源管理系统。

Thames Water's announcement of a hosepipe ban is framed as a necessary step to "protect everyone," but it is, in reality, a textbook case of structural violence. The company is shifting the responsibility for systemic resource depletion from strategic management failure onto the daily behavior of 23 million individuals.

The claim that demand has increased by "10%," compared to the scale of "adding an entire city of Manchester," is a weaponized cognitive entry point. It manufactures a crisis of consumption while remaining silent on crumbling infrastructure, network leakages, and the long-term absence of capital investment in water resilience. When the gap between Potential and Actual capacity widens, the company resorts to cultural violence—using the narratives of "environmentalism" and "shared sacrifice" to make the deprivation of water rights seem "just."

This is a masterclass in complicity. The government, water companies, and meteorological bodies co-construct a narrative of "climate extremes" as an irresistible force, masking structural violence under the guise of natural disaster. In this masculine-centric management logic, the solution is always to issue commands, define restricted zones, and impose limits, rather than questioning the primal flaws in power and resource distribution. The hosepipe ban isn't firefighting; it is a performative concession designed to hide a collapsing system.
