关税战：一场关于“谁在买单”的认知scamTariff Wars: A Cognitive Scam of Cost Shifting

国际 结构层 · 文化层 The New York Times ↗ 2026-07-20 § 链接
贸易战不是经济博弈，而是通过结构性暴力转移成本的权力游戏。
Trade wars are not economic games, but power plays shifting costs via structural violence.

所谓的“关税政策在按预期发挥作用”，这个“预期”在 Jamieson Greer 的叙事里是国家竞争力的回归，但在 Galtung 的暴力三角里，这不过是典型的 structural violence。关税本质上是一次成本的强制转移：政府通过在认知入口制造“贸易战”的宏大叙事，掩盖了成本最终由底层消费者承担的事实。这是一种极其粗暴的武器化表达——将复杂的经济剥削包装成爱国主义的胜利。

在这场博弈中，特朗普政府扮演的是规则制定者，他们通过定义什么是“成功”来夺取解释权。当通货膨胀让普通美国人生活拮据时，他们并不解决 Actual 状态（物价上涨），而是通过重新定义 Potential 状态（所谓的制造业回流）来抵消公众的感知。这和某些科技公司做不了芯片就宣布新定律的逻辑如出一辙：现实不配合，就换一把尺子，然后宣布自己赢了。

最值得警惕的是这种共谋机制。权力中心通过密集投放“强力谈判”的形象，诱导部分群体产生一种“我们在赢”的错觉，从而在潜意识中成为这场成本转移的共谋者。他们被告知在为国家利益而牺牲，但实际上，被牺牲的是他们自己的购买力，而获利的是能够通过贸易壁垒垄断市场的资本寡头。这种叙事将个体对生存成本的焦虑，转化为对外部敌人的仇恨，完成了从经济压迫到文化暴力的闭环。

The claim that tariff policies are "working as intended" is a textbook case of structural violence. In Jamieson Greer's narrative, "intent" means national competitiveness; in Galtung's Violence Triangle, it means the forced transfer of costs. Tariffs are a weaponized expression: the government uses a grand narrative of "trade wars" at the cognitive entrance to mask the fact that the burden falls squarely on bottom-tier consumers.

In this game, the administration acts as the rule-maker, seizing the power of interpretation by defining what constitutes "success." When inflation erodes the affordability of everyday life, they don't fix the Actual state (rising prices); instead, they redefine the Potential state (so-called reshoring) to neutralize public perception. It is the same logic as a tech company announcing a new "law" when they fail to build a chip: if reality doesn't cooperate, change the ruler and declare victory.

Most alarming is the mechanism of complicity. By flooding the discourse with images of "strong negotiation," the power center tricks certain groups into a delusion of winning. They are induced to become co-conspirators in their own exploitation, believing they are sacrificing for national interest while their actual purchasing power is gutted. The real winners are the capital oligarchs who use trade barriers to monopolize markets. This narrative transforms the individual's anxiety over survival into hatred for an external enemy, completing the loop from structural oppression to cultural violence.
