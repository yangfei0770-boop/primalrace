所谓的“拘留沙漠”不过是暴力机器的扩容计划The 'Detention Desert' is Just a Blueprint for Expanding the Violence Machine

国际 结构层 · 文化层 The New York Times ↗ 2026-07-23 § 链接
将资源匮乏区定义为“沙漠”，是为了在其中合法地建立监狱。
Defining a lack of facilities as a 'desert' is a narrative trick to justify building prisons.

ICE 所谓的“拘留沙漠” (Detention Desert) 是一个典型的 weaponized 叙事。在权力者的字典里，当一个地区缺乏囚禁移民的设施时，那不是一种人道主义的空白，而是一个亟待填补的“资源缺口”。通过将这种状态定义为“沙漠”，ICE 成功地将扩建拘留中心的行为伪装成了某种“基础设施建设”或“填补空白”的行政逻辑，从而掩盖了其本质：为大规模驱逐计划准备更多的肉体禁锢空间。

这件事揭示了 structural violence 的高效运作方式：政府不直接买仓库（因为太显眼，容易引发 PR 危机），而是与 GEO Group 这种私人监狱巨头共谋 (complicity)。私人公司负责提供物理上的禁锢空间，政府负责提供被囚禁的肉体，双方在一个闭环的利益链条中通过贸易“人口”来换取利润与政治筹码。这种公私合营的模式让暴力变得极其隐蔽——它不再是某种激烈的政治冲突，而变成了合同谈判、床位数 (beds) 的统计以及对“友好地区”的筛选。

最讽刺的是，这种筛选机制本身就是一种文化暴力的延伸。所谓的“友好地区”，就是那些元暴力 (meta violence) 根深蒂固、对弱势族群缺乏共情、且习惯于将“秩序”置于“人权”之上的地方。在这些地方，禁锢他人的行为被认为是维护社区安全的“正确表达”。

Potential 与 Actual 的差额在进一步扩大。移民们本可拥有的生存权与自由，被精准地量化成了 1,400 个床位。当暴力被转化为一种高效的物流与资产管理时，我们面对的不再是法律，而是一台巨大的、以人口为原材料的收割机。

ICE's use of the term "Detention Desert" is a textbook example of a weaponized narrative. In the lexicon of power, a region lacking facilities to imprison migrants is not a humanitarian void, but a "resource gap" waiting to be filled. By framing this state as a "desert," ICE transforms the act of expanding detention centers into a banal administrative task of "infrastructure development," masking the raw truth: they are simply preparing more physical cages for a mass deportation campaign.

This reveals the cold efficiency of structural violence. Instead of buying warehouses—which is too conspicuous and risks a PR nightmare—the government enters into a state of complicity with private prison giants like the GEO Group. The private sector provides the cages; the government provides the bodies. Together, they trade in human misery for profit and political capital. This public-private partnership renders violence invisible, turning it into a matter of contract negotiations, "bed counts," and the strategic selection of "friendly" regions.

The most cynical part is the selection mechanism itself, an extension of cultural violence. A "friendly" region is simply one where meta violence is deeply ingrained, where empathy for the marginalized is absent, and where "order" is weaponized against "human rights." In these spaces, the act of imprisoning others is presented as a "just expression" of community security.

The gap between Potential and Actual is widening. The fundamental rights to life and liberty of these migrants are being quantified into 1,400 beds. When violence is translated into the language of logistics and asset management, we are no longer dealing with law, but with a massive harvesting machine that treats human beings as raw material.
