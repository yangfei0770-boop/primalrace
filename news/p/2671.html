蟑螂的命名艺术与权力结构的暴力回弹The Art of Cockroach Naming and the Recoil of Power

国际 直接层 · 结构层 · 文化层 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-22 § 链接
当弱势者将自嘲武器化为身份，暴力就成了权力最无能的表达。
When the marginalized weaponize self-deprecation into identity, violence becomes the most impotent expression of power.

起名为“蟑螂人民党”(CJP) 是一个极其精明的表达博弈。在元暴力的叙事里，蟑螂意味着低贱、肮脏、必须被清除的客体。但这群年轻人通过自我定义，将这种被动属性转化为主动的身份政治：蟑螂最核心的特质是“无法被彻底消灭”。这种命名直接拆穿了莫迪政府试图通过暴力维持的“秩序”幻象——你越是用催泪瓦斯和警棍试图清理，就越是证明了这种生命力的顽强。这就是一种典型的、针对结构性压迫的“真.最优解表达”。

而莫迪政府的反应则极其刻板。从警棍袭击到拘捕反对派领袖 Rahul Gandhi，这套动作是典型的直接暴力 (direct violence) 试图掩盖结构暴力 (structural violence)。教育系统的考试丑闻是结构性的盘剥，而警察的暴力则是为了维持这种盘剥而不得不启动的物理清理。当 Kiren Rijiju 还在用“青年是优先级”这种 weaponized 的官话进行文化掩盖时，一个在呼吸机上的抗议者已经成了这套叙事最讽刺的注脚。

Rahul Gandhi 的被捕在某种程度上是一次政治表演，但它揭示了印度当前一个危险的共谋逻辑：体制内的反对派与体制外的激进青年在此时达成了短暂的利益站队。这种结盟不是因为价值观完全一致，而是在面对一个日益垄断解释权的强权政府时，为了生存而产生的必然博弈。当“自尊”成为唯一的入场券，这场存在性战争的战场已经从议会转移到了街头。

Naming themselves the "Cockroach Janta Party" (CJP) is a brilliant move in the game of expression. In the masculine-centric narrative of meta-violence, cockroaches are objects of filth and lowliness, meant to be exterminated. However, these youths have flipped this passive attribute into active identity politics: the defining trait of a cockroach is that it is "impossible to eradicate." This naming directly dismantles the illusion of "order" the Modi government tries to maintain. The more they use tear gas and batons to "cleanse," the more they prove the resilience of this movement. This is a classic "True Optimal Expression" against structural oppression.

Modi's response is predictably clichéd. From baton charges to the detention of Rahul Gandhi, this is direct violence attempting to mask structural violence. The education system's testing scandals are a form of structural exploitation; the police brutality is merely the physical cleanup required to maintain that exploitation. While Kiren Rijiju employs weaponized rhetoric, claiming "youth are our priority," a protester on a ventilator serves as the most ironic footnote to that cultural mask.

Gandhi's detention is partly a political performance, but it reveals a dangerous logic of complicity: a temporary alliance between establishment opposition and radical youth. This alignment isn't necessarily based on shared values, but is a necessary gamble for survival against a government monopolizing the power of interpretation. When "self-respect" becomes the only ticket to the game, the existential war has officially shifted from the parliament to the streets.
