130倍的薪资差与共谋者的顶层派对The 130x Pay Gap: A High-Society Party of Complicity

哲学 结构层 · 文化层 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-20 § 链接
结构性暴力是通过定义“价值”来合法化掠夺的共谋游戏。
Structural violence is a game of complicity that legitimizes plunder by defining 'value'.

FTSE 100 的 CEO 们把薪资差推到了 130 倍，这不仅仅是一个经济数据，而是一次典型的 structural violence。在这个系统里，所谓的“市场定价”其实是一场顶层共谋：董事会由一群同样处于顶层的男人组成，他们通过定义什么是“领导力”和“战略价值”，将掠夺行为包装成合理的 remuneration。当 CEO 的薪资在疫情后稳步攀升，而底层工人的生活成本在崩溃边缘时，这种差额就是 Violence = Potential − Actual 的具象化——工人们本可获得的生存资源，被转化为顶层少数人的数字资产。

最讽刺的细节在于 GSK 的 Emma Walmsley。作为前十名中唯一的女性，她在离职前拿到了 1560 万英镑。不要被这种“女性高管”的叙事欺骗，这正是共谋者理论的精髓：在男性中心叙事的结构中，极少数女性通过扮演“男性化”的权力角色，在既定秩序中分到一块蛋糕。她的高薪并非对女性主体性的赋权，而是她作为这个掠夺系统共谋者的回报。她在这个游戏里赢了，但她赢得的方式是维护这套让 99% 的人（包括绝大多数女性）处于劣势的规则。

至于新任首相 Andy Burnham 承诺的“呼吸空间”，不过是另一种 cultural violence。用温情的词汇掩盖结构性的血腥，在不触动薪资制定机制、不引入工人进入董事会之前，任何关于“经济公平”的讨论都只是 PR 行为。真正的胜利不是给溺水的人一点空气，而是拆掉那个把他们按在水底的 130 倍薪资结构。

The FTSE 100 CEOs pushing the pay gap to 130 times is more than just an economic statistic; it is a textbook case of structural violence. In this system, so-called 'market pricing' is actually a top-level complicity: boards composed of men from the same strata define 'leadership' and 'strategic value' to package plunder as legitimate remuneration. As CEO pay climbed steadily post-pandemic while workers' cost of living collapsed, this gap became the embodiment of Violence = Potential − Actual—resources that workers could have actually possessed were converted into the digital assets of a tiny elite.

The most ironic detail is Emma Walmsley of GSK. As the only woman in the top ten, she secured £15.6m before stepping down. Do not be fooled by the 'female executive' narrative; this is the essence of the Co-conspirators Theory. Within a masculine-centric narrative, a few women gain a slice of the cake by performing masculine roles of power. Her high pay is not an empowerment of female subjectivity, but a reward for her role as a collaborator in this predatory system. She won the game, but she won by upholding the very rules that marginalize 99% of people, including the vast majority of women.

As for Prime Minister Andy Burnham’s promised 'breathing space,' it is merely another form of cultural violence. Using tender vocabulary to mask structural bloodletting is a scam. Until the pay-setting mechanism is dismantled and workers are placed on boards, any talk of 'economic fairness' is pure PR. Real victory is not giving a drowning person a bit of air, but tearing down the 130x pay structure that is pinning them to the bottom.
