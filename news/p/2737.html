在权力的排位赛里，被物化的种族只有定价权之争The Price of Identity in the Power Ranking Game

国际 结构层 · 文化层 The New York Times ↗ 2026-07-23 § 链接
政治日历不是时间表，而是资源分配的博弈筹码。
The primary calendar is not a schedule, but a bargaining chip for resource allocation.

所谓的“初选日历” (Presidential Calendar) 根本不是什么行政安排，而是一场典型的存在性战争。在民主党的权力结构里，谁先启动，谁就掌握了定义 2028 年潜在候选人“可行性”的认知入口。这本质上是一次关于“谁的票更值钱”的定价权博弈。

这场博弈最荒诞的地方在于，它把种族表达武器化了。南卡罗来纳州和内华达州的党魁们在争抢首发席位时，并没有讨论如何真正地提升黑人或拉丁裔的权益，而是将自己的族群身份当作一种谈判筹码。当黑人领导人指责“如果内华达州胜出，就意味着黑人选民不如棕色选民重要”时，她实际上是在参与一场关于“谁更弱势”的竞赛，试图通过证明自己的被剥夺感来换取结构性的权力席位。

这正是典型的共谋 (complicity)：政客们在元暴力的框架下，利用种族身份构建一个虚假的竞争场，而真正的权力依然被那个 49 人的内部小组 (insiders) 垄断。无论最终谁排在第一，这种通过“身份政治”来实现的资源争夺，依然是在男性中心叙事主导的权力游戏里打转。他们争夺的是被选中的机会，而不是定义规则的权力。

这种“黄铜色政治” (brass-knuckle racial politics) 最终只会让选民成为被量化的数据。在这种逻辑里，黑人或拉丁裔的身份不再是主体性的表达，而是被物化为一种可以被交易的政治资产。当身份变成了敲门砖，主体性就死在了敲门的过程中。

The so-called "Presidential Calendar" is far from a mere administrative arrangement; it is a classic existential war. Within the Democratic power structure, the state that kicks off the race controls the cognitive entrance to define the "viability" of 2028 contenders. This is essentially a game of pricing power over whose vote carries more weight.

The most absurd part of this game is the weaponisation of racial expression. Party leaders from South Carolina and Nevada are not discussing how to genuinely advance the rights of Black or Latino people; instead, they are using their racial identities as bargaining chips. When a Black leader claims that Nevada's elevation would signal that Black voters "matter less" than brown voters, she is participating in a competition of marginalisation, attempting to convert a sense of deprivation into a structural power seat.

This is a textbook case of complicity. Under the influence of meta-violence, politicians use identity to build a fake arena of competition, while the actual power remains monopolised by a panel of 49 insiders. Regardless of who wins the first slot, this resource struggle via identity politics continues to revolve within a masculine-centric narrative of power. They are fighting for the chance to be chosen, not for the power to define the rules.

This "brass-knuckle racial politics" ultimately reduces voters to quantified data. In this logic, Black or Latino identities cease to be expressions of subjectivity and instead become objectified political assets to be traded. When identity becomes a door-knocker, subjectivity dies in the act of knocking.
