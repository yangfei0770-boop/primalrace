用“威慑”为名，在血肉与账单中制造 Forever WarManufacturing a Forever War in the Name of 'Deterrence'

国际 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-22 § 链接
战争叙事是最高级的武器化，它将具体的人命量化为抽象的预算。
War narratives are the ultimate weaponization, quantifying human life into abstract budget lines.

Hegseth 在参议院的表演是一次典型的认知入口争夺战。当战争成本从 300 亿飙升至 375 亿，且美军伤亡数字在增加时，五角大楼并没有在讨论“失败”，而是在通过重新定义叙事来掩盖 Structural Violence。将追加 880 亿预算定义为“必要的威慑”，本质上是将一个失控的博弈过程包装成一种“能力建设”的 scam。

Patty Murray 揭露了这套叙事武器的低劣之处：理由从“预警威胁”到“政权更迭”再到“拆除核计划”，最后居然变成了“重新开放”一个原本就开放的霍尔穆兹海峡。这种叙事的快速迭代证明了，所谓的“战争理由”根本不是事实，而是一套为了获取资金和权力而实时生成的 Weaponized Expression。在这些男人主导的权力博弈中，士兵的死亡被简化为“补给不足”的理由，而海峡的关闭则成了索要更多支票的筹码。

这场战争是典型的 Meta Violence 产物。从 Hegseth 到 Caine，这些处于权力顶端的男性通过垄断解释权，将国家机器转化为一个巨大的消耗性工具。他们共谋地将“准备战争”等同于“创造和平”，从而在逻辑上把永无止境的军费扩张合法化。在这种 Masculine-centric Narrative 中，真实的生命个体（无论是美军士兵还是伊朗平民）都被客体化为数字，而真正的“胜利”仅仅是指五角大楼的预算表能继续增长。

最讽刺的是，当被要求给出关于海峡收费的“直截了当的答案”时，将军选择了回避。因为在他们的博弈模型里，真相并不重要，重要的是维持一个“必须由我来解决”的危机状态。这就是制造可能性的艺术：通过制造一个永远无法通过资金填平的黑洞，确保权力机构永远拥有支配资源的优先权。

Hegseth's performance before the Senate is a classic battle for the cognitive entry point. As the war cost spirals from $30bn to $37.5bn and casualties mount, the Pentagon isn't discussing 'failure'; they are redefining the narrative to mask Structural Violence. Framing an $88bn supplemental request as 'necessary deterrence' is essentially packaging a spiraling game of existential war as a 'capability build' scam.

Patty Murray exposed the crudeness of this weaponized expression: the rationale shifted from 'pre-empting threats' to 'regime change,' then to 'dismantling nuclear programs,' and finally to 'reopening' a strait that was already open. This rapid iteration proves that the 'justification' for war is not a fact, but a set of Weaponized Expressions generated in real-time to secure funding and power. In this masculine-centric game, soldiers' deaths are reduced to justifications for 'replenishing munitions,' and the closure of a strait becomes a bargaining chip for more checks.

This conflict is a pure product of Meta Violence. From Hegseth to Caine, the men at the top maintain a monopoly over the power of interpretation, transforming the state machine into a massive tool of consumption. They act as complicity partners, equating 'preparing for war' with 'creating peace' to legitimize endless military expansion. In this narrative, individual lives—whether US soldiers or Iranian civilians—are objectified into statistics, while 'victory' is redefined as the continuous growth of the Pentagon's budget.

Most ironically, when pressed for a 'straight answer' about tolls in the strait, the General evaded. In their game, truth is irrelevant; what matters is maintaining a state of crisis that only they can 'solve.' This is the art of manufacturing possibilities: by creating a black hole that no amount of funding can fill, they ensure the power structure retains the priority of resource control.
