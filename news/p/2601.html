Syrskyi 的“奶酪”陷阱与权力共谋的剧本Syrskyi’s Cheese Trap and the Script of Power Complicity

国际 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-20 § 链接
所谓的“听取民意”，不过是权力在寻找一个更顺从的执行工具。
So-called 'listening to the public' is merely power seeking a more compliant tool of execution.

Syrskyi 很快会被扫地出门，但这绝不是什么“民主的胜利”或“民意的回归”。在权力博弈的棋盘上，抗议者挥舞的奶酪刨（cheese graters）只是这场权力置换的背景板。真正的战争发生在 Zelenskyy、Syrskyi 和 Fedorov 之间——这是一场关于谁能掌控战争机器、谁在定义“胜利”的存在性战争。

Syrskyi 试图用一种典型的 masculine-centric 叙事来挽回局面：他谈论“战略”、谈论“前线”、将冲突定义为“两个机构的正常分歧”，并试图用“关注全球胜利”这种宏大叙事来掩盖个人的权力贪婪。这是一种极其拙劣的武器化表达，试图通过将自己塑造为“专注于战争的纯粹军人”来要求豁免权。但这种叙事在权力顶端失效了，因为他不再是那个能提供最优解的工具。

最讽刺的是军队内部爆料的那个“理想接任者”画像：要有名气、要受欢迎，但绝不能有政治野心，必须“听总统的话，做总统想做的事”。这揭露了这套体制的本质——它不需要一个真正的战略家，而需要一个完美的共谋者。Fedorov 的“现代化”改革触动了军队内部的利益网络，而 Syrskyi 则通过奖励忠诚者来构建自己的微型权力王国。当这种结构性暴力开始影响战场结果时，Zelenskyy 才会通过一次表演性的“听取民意”来完成清理。

这场闹剧的结局大概率是：一个更听话的将军上位，Fedorov 可能会以某种形式回归作为 PR 遮羞布，而那些在街头抗议的人会被告知他们“赢了”。但这只是 structural violence 的一次换皮，只要权力依然追求的是“绝对的顺从”而非“公正的表达”，那么无论谁在指挥，士兵依然只是被消耗的工具。

Syrskyi is likely headed for the exit, but this is far from a 'victory for democracy' or a 'return to public will.' On the chessboard of power, the cheese graters waved by protesters are merely backdrops for a strategic replacement. The real war is being fought between Zelenskyy, Syrskyi, and Fedorov—an existential war over who controls the war machine and who defines 'victory.'

Syrskyi attempted to salvage his position using a classic masculine-centric narrative: he spoke of 'strategy,' the 'frontline,' and framed the conflict as a 'normal disagreement between institutions,' attempting to cloak his personal power grab in the grand narrative of 'global victory.' This is a clumsy attempt at weaponizing expression, trying to claim immunity by posing as a 'pure soldier focused on war.' But this narrative failed at the top; he is no longer the tool providing the optimal solution.

Most ironic is the 'ideal replacement' described by military sources: someone famous and popular, but devoid of political ambition, who 'listens to the president and does what he wants.' This exposes the essence of the system—it doesn't need a strategist; it needs a perfect complicit. Fedorov’s 'modernization' reforms threatened the internal interest networks, while Syrskyi built his own micro-kingdom by rewarding loyalty. When this structural violence began to impair battlefield results, Zelenskyy decided to perform a 'listening to the public' act to clear the deck.

The finale will likely be this: a more obedient general takes over, Fedorov may return in some capacity as a PR shield, and the street protesters will be told they 'won.' In reality, this is just a re-skinning of structural violence. As long as power demands 'absolute compliance' rather than 'Just Expressions,' the soldiers remain nothing more than consumable tools, regardless of who leads.
