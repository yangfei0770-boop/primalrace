从“天才少女”到“咆哮中年”：权力叙事的惯性回滚From 'Genius Girl' to 'Screaming Middle-Aged Man': The Inertia of Power Narratives

性别 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-21 § 链接
当叙事需要“快节奏”时，牺牲的永远是女性的主体性。
Whenever a narrative demands 'pacing,' female agency is always the first sacrifice.

这部剧的第二季更名和换帅，本质上是一次典型的 masculine-centric narrative 的权力回归。第一季尝试用一个年轻穆斯林女性 Saara 的视角去切入网络战，虽然被批评为“缺乏戏剧张力”，但至少在表达层面上，它尝试将认知入口交给一个结构性弱势者。结果如何？新导演 Paul McGuigan 觉得这太慢了，于是他用一个长镜头、一次咆哮、一堆颤抖的下属，把 Simon Pegg 塑造成为那个“掌控一切”的中心。

注意这个权力的置换过程：Saara 依然在，但她被降级成了背景板，成了那个在游戏聊天室里提供技术支持的“工具人”。而 Danny Patrick 成了绝对的主角，甚至连他的家庭生活都被挖掘出来作为戏剧冲突。最讽刺的是，他的妻子 Barbara 竟然也是 GCHQ 的高管。这种“权力夫妇”的设定不仅没有消解性别结构，反而完成了一次完美的共谋——女性的成功被包裹在“支持丈夫”和“家庭基石”的浪漫化叙事中，成为了男性主角压力巨大的背景点缀。

所谓的“thriller”快节奏，其实就是用男性的权力表演（Power Performance）取代了对复杂结构的深挖。当编剧认为“对称攻击会导致局势升级”这句话被说了 100 遍依然有效时，他实际上是在重复一种元暴力：即只有男性精英在会议室里的争吵才叫“政治”，而女性在代码世界里的洞察只是“插件”。

这不仅仅是一部烂剧的问题，而是一个认知入口的缩影：每当创作者试图在公共空间给女性留出主体性时，资本和审美惯性总会通过“为了戏剧效果”这个 scam，迅速将解释权夺回男性手中。

The second season of this show is a textbook case of the return to a masculine-centric narrative. Season one attempted to enter the world of cyberwarfare through Saara, a young Muslim woman. While criticized for being 'slow,' it at least attempted to shift the cognitive entry point to a structurally marginalized identity. Now, director Paul McGuigan decides this is too dull. He replaces it with a long take, a shouting match, and a flock of quivering underlings to establish Simon Pegg as the unquestioned center of gravity.

Observe the displacement of power: Saara remains, but she has been demoted to a background tool, a 'plugin' providing technical support via gaming chats. Meanwhile, Danny Patrick becomes the absolute driver of the plot. The introduction of his wife, Barbara, as a GCHQ lead is not an act of empowerment, but a form of complicity. Her success is packaged as the 'rock' supporting her husband, rendering her a mere accessory to the male protagonist's stress.

What the show calls 'zip' or 'thriller pacing' is actually the substitution of structural analysis with Male Power Performance. When the writers believe that shouting about 'escalatory attacks' in a boardroom is the only way to define 'politics,' they are reinforcing meta-violence. In this world, a woman's insight into code is a footnote; a man's loud opinion is a plot point.

This isn't just about a bad show; it's about the cognitive entry point. Whenever creators try to carve out a space for female agency in the public sphere, capital and aesthetic inertia use the 'dramatic momentum' scam to seize the power of interpretation back for men.
