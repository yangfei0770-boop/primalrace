巨型沙发上的客体化表演Objectification Performance on the Giant Sofa

性别 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-22 § 链接
所谓的“不适应”并非能力问题，而是女性在男本位叙事模版中的主体性失效。
The so-called 'mismatch' is not a lack of skill, but the failure of female subjectivity within a masculine-centric narrative template.

Claudia Winkleman 的失败被描述为“紧张”或“节奏不对”，但本质上，这又是一次典型的女性在男性中心叙事 (masculine-centric narrative) 模版中寻找最优解而不得的博弈。那个所谓的“巨型沙发” (giant sofa) 不是家具，而是一个结构性的权力场域。深夜访谈节目的定义权、主持逻辑和观众期待，全是由 Carson 到 Norton 这一系列男性定义并垄断的。在这个场域里，男性主持人的“犀利”被视为掌控力，而女性在同一套逻辑下尝试时，要么被评价为“太吵”，要么被指责为“缺乏掌控”。

有趣的是，文中提到唯一成功的女性案例是 Mrs Merton，但她必须通过扮演一个“伪天真”的 comic character 才能生存。这意味着女性只有在扮演一个被定义好的、非主体性的角色时，才能在男本位叙事中获得通行证。这正是典型的“假.最优解表达”：通过扮演他者认可的角色换取短期生存，代价是主体性的死亡。

Davina McCall 在播客这种去中心化的新媒介中获得成功，证明了当女性脱离那个由男性共谋者 (complicit) 建立的传统电视权力结构，不再试图在“巨型沙发”上证明自己能像男人一样主持节目时，她们才能找到真.最优解。所谓的“不适应”，其实是身体在诚实地拒绝进入一个将女性客体化的叙事陷阱。

Claudia Winkleman's failure is framed as 'nervousness' or 'poor rhythm,' but it is essentially another case of women failing to find a true optimal expression while gambling within a masculine-centric narrative template. That 'giant sofa' is not a piece of furniture; it is a structural field of power. The definition, logic, and audience expectations of late-night chatshows have been monopolized by a lineage of men from Carson to Norton.

In this arena, a male host's 'sharpness' is viewed as mastery, whereas a woman attempting the same logic is either dismissed as 'too loud' or criticized for 'lacking control.' Interestingly, the only successful female example mentioned is Mrs Merton, who had to operate under the cover of a 'faux-naif' comic character. This proves that women are only granted a pass in the masculine narrative when they perform a predefined, non-subjective role—a classic 'false optimal expression' where survival is traded for the death of subjectivity.

Davina McCall's success in the decentralized medium of podcasts proves that when women exit the traditional TV power structure built by male complicity and stop trying to prove they can host 'like a man' on that giant sofa, they finally find their true optimal expression. The so-called 'mismatch' is actually the body honestly rejecting an invitation to enter a narrative trap that objectifies women.
