巴克莱的“被误导”与男权共谋的避风港Barclays' 'Misleading' Narrative and the Haven of Masculine Complicity

性别 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-23 § 链接
所谓的“被误导”，不过是权力阶层在共谋中互给的免罪符。
Being 'misled' is merely a mutual immunity grant within the circles of power.

巴克莱银行在面对杰斯·斯塔利（Jes Staley）与爱泼斯坦（Jeffrey Epstein）深厚关系时的反应，是典型的 structural violence 掩盖下的共谋表演。银行声称被 Staley “误导”了，这意味着一个顶级金融机构在聘请 CEO 时，竟然愿意在没有 due diligence 的情况下，仅仅因为对方是一个“同类”，就全盘接受其关于人贩子好友的口头承诺。这不是能力不足，而是选择性失明。

在男性中心叙事（meta violence）的权力场中，这种“信任”是一种特权。Staley 和 Epstein 的关系不仅仅是私人友谊，而是一套基于资源交换、性剥削和权力互认的男性共谋网络。巴克莱董事会成员在面对 Staley 时，潜意识里将其视为“我们的人”，因此他们不需要通过证据来验证事实，只需要通过“身份认同”来确认安全。这种共谋的回报是极高的：它维持了精英阶层的稳定性，确保权力在同一类人手中流动，而那些被爱泼斯坦 trafficking 的女性，在这一套精英共谋的逻辑中，甚至连被当作“问题”讨论的资格都没有，她们只是这个权力游戏中的背景噪音。

Elizabeth Warren 的质询点出了一个关键：银行牌照是 privilege，而这种特权本应基于管理层的 fitness。但现实是，在金融资本的顶端，fitness 的定义往往就是“能否完美地融入这个共谋圈子”。巴克莱试图用“新信息出现”来洗白，这不过是文化暴力（cultural violence）的常规操作——通过重新定义叙事，将结构性的纵容包装成个体的欺骗。

这场博弈的本质是：一个男权共谋的黑盒被法律强行撬开了一个缝隙。但只要这种“只要是自己人就无需尽职调查”的元暴力逻辑依然存在，即便 Staley 滚蛋，这个系统依然在为下一个 Epstein 预留席位。

Barclays' reaction to Jes Staley's deep ties with Jeffrey Epstein is a textbook performance of complicity masking structural violence. The bank claims it was 'misled' by Staley, implying that a leading financial institution, when hiring a CEO, was content to waive due diligence and accept a verbal promise regarding a friendship with a sex trafficker simply because the candidate was 'one of them.' This isn't a failure of competence; it is a calculated selective blindness.

Within the masculine-centric narrative—the meta violence—this kind of 'trust' is a privilege. The relationship between Staley and Epstein was not merely a private friendship, but a network of masculine complicity built on resource exchange, sexual exploitation, and mutual recognition of power. The Barclays board, viewing Staley as 'one of us,' didn't need evidence to verify facts; they only needed 'identity alignment' to confirm safety. The payoff for this complicity is immense: it maintains the stability of the elite and ensures power flows among a specific breed, while the women trafficked by Epstein remain mere background noise, not even qualifying as 'problems' in this logic of elite collusion.

Elizabeth Warren’s inquiry highlights a crucial point: banking licenses are a privilege contingent on the 'fitness' of management. In reality, at the peak of financial capital, 'fitness' is often defined as the ability to seamlessly integrate into this circle of complicity. Barclays' attempt to whitewash the situation by citing 'newly emerged information' is a standard move of cultural violence—redefining the narrative to package structural indulgence as individual deception.

At its core, this is a case of a masculine complicity black box being pried open by legal force. However, as long as the meta violence logic—'no due diligence needed for our own'—persists, the system will continue to reserve a seat for the next Epstein, even after Staley is gone.
