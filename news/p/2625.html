赞美作为另一种形式的客体化Compliments as Another Form of Objectification

性别 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-21 § 链接
当一个人的主体性被简化为“功能性奇迹”时，赞美就是一种温和的剥夺。
When subjectivity is reduced to 'functional miracles,' praise becomes a gentle form of deprivation.

这篇文章表面上在写一个 52 岁女性在 80 岁继母面前享受“被崇拜”的快感，实际上揭示了一场极其微小但典型的存在性战争。在这种互动中，继母的赞美并非基于对 Zoe 独立人格的认可，而是一种基于“生物墙”差异的投射。当继母惊叹于“从车里快速走出来”或“打开一个僵硬的门”时，她实际上是在将 Zoe 简化为一个拥有高效生理功能的生物样本。

这种赞美是 weaponized 的。它通过建立一个极低的功能性阈值，诱导 Zoe 进入一种“假.最优解表达”：通过展示简单的身体机能来获取短期情绪价值。在这种博弈中，Zoe 的主体性在不知不觉中被让渡了——她开始通过搬动花盆来“表演”强健，从而在继母构建的叙事中获得一个“神奇个体”的席位。这本质上是一种温和的客体化，将一个成年人的存在价值锚定在“能正常呼吸、行走和开门”这些基础生物指标上。

最讽刺的是，这种共谋在文中被包装成一种温馨的家庭和谐。继母通过赞美来确认自己的衰老与匮乏，而 Zoe 通过被赞美来填补某种心理缺口。这种互利共谋掩盖了其底层的元暴力：在传统的家庭叙事中，女性的价值往往被定义为“被观看”或“被定义”的客体。即便此时定义权在继母手中，这种通过赞美来完成的“定义”过程，依然在重复那套将女性身体作为评价指标的旧逻辑。当 Zoe 觉得自己在“赢”的时候，她其实正处于主体性死亡的边缘。

On the surface, this piece describes a 52-year-old woman enjoying the thrill of being 'idolized' by her 80-year-old stepmother. In reality, it reveals a tiny but classic existential war. The stepmother's praise is not based on a recognition of Zoe's independent personality, but on a projection of the differences across the biological wall. When the stepmother marvels at 'getting out of the car quickly' or 'opening a stiff door,' she is effectively reducing Zoe to a biological specimen with efficient physiological functions.

This praise is weaponized. By establishing an incredibly low functional threshold, it lures Zoe into a 'fake optimal expression': performing simple physical tasks to gain short-term emotional rewards. In this game, Zoe's subjectivity is silently surrendered—she begins to 'perform' strength by heaving plant pots just to secure a seat as a 'magic entity' within the narrative constructed by her stepmother. This is essentially a mild form of objectification, anchoring an adult's existential value to basic biological markers like breathing, walking, and opening doors.

The irony is that this complicity is packaged as warm family harmony. The stepmother confirms her own decay and lack through praise, while Zoe fills a psychological void by being praised. This mutual complicity masks the underlying meta-violence: in traditional domestic narratives, a woman's value is often defined as an object to be 'observed' or 'defined.' Even with the power of definition currently held by the stepmother, the process of 'defining' through praise continues to repeat the old logic of using the female body as a metric. While Zoe feels she is 'winning,' she is actually drifting toward the death of her subjectivity.
