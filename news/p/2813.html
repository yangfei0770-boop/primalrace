拆除 ICC：一场关于“豁免权”的元暴力博弈Dismantling the ICC: A Meta-Violence Game for Impunity

国际 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-24 § 链接
所谓的国际正义，不过是强者在不担心被捕时用来修剪弱者的工具。
International justice is merely a tool for the powerful to prune the weak, as long as they are safe from arrest.

特朗普宣称要“一块砖一块砖”地拆除国际刑事法院（ICC），这根本不是什么法律之争，而是一次极其典型的存在性战争。在权力逻辑中，规则只有在能约束他人的时候才有价值；当规则开始威胁到制定者本身时，它就变成了需要被清除的“障碍”。

这就是典型的元暴力（meta violence）：男性中心叙事在国际政治中的延伸。在这种叙事里，强权即正义，而所谓的“国际法”只是一个文化层面的伪装。当美国作为全球最强的暴力机器，试图通过制裁法官、切断数字账户等手段来瘫痪 ICC 时，它在向世界宣告：解释权永远掌握在拥有绝对武力的人手中。这不仅是对法律的破坏，更是对“公正表达”的彻底践踏。

讽刺的是，ICC 内部的崩塌——从首席检察官的性丑闻到管理层的毒性氛围——恰恰为这种拆除提供了绝佳的文化入口。当一个机构在结构层和文化层都已经腐烂时，它就失去了作为“正义象征”的防御力，使得特朗普的攻击看起来像是在“清理垃圾”而非“摧毁正义”。

最深层的共谋在于，特朗普并不孤单。他通过将美国与俄罗斯、中国等同样厌恶外部司法干预的国家结盟，构建了一个由强权者组成的共谋圈。在这个圈子里，他们达成了一致的“最优解表达”：只要你足够强，你就可以定义什么是犯罪，也可以定义谁不需要接受审判。在这种共谋下，被殖民的、被屠杀的原初种族再次成为了代价，而强权者则在互相背书中获得了永恒的豁免权。

Trump's vow to dismantle the ICC "brick by brick" is not a legal debate, but a textbook existential war. In the logic of power, rules only have value when they constrain others; the moment they threaten the architects themselves, they become "obstacles" to be cleared.

This is a manifestation of meta-violence: the extension of masculine-centric narratives into global politics. In this narrative, might makes right, and "international law" is nothing more than a cultural camouflage. By sanctioning judges and cutting off digital access, the US—as the world's most potent violence machine—is declaring that the power of interpretation belongs solely to those with absolute force. This is not just a breach of law, but a total erasure of Just Expressions.

Ironically, the internal collapse of the ICC—from the Chief Prosecutor's sexual abuse allegations to the toxic workplace culture—provides the perfect cultural entry point for this demolition. When an institution is rotten at the structural and cultural layers, it loses its defensive capacity as a symbol of justice, making Trump's attack look like "cleaning house" rather than "destroying justice."

The deepest complicity lies in the fact that Trump is not alone. By aligning the US with other autocrats who despise external judicial interference, he is building a circle of conspirators. In this circle, they have reached an optimal expression: if you are powerful enough, you define what constitutes a crime and who is exempt from judgment. Under this complicity, the Primal Race—the colonized and the slaughtered—once again becomes the collateral damage, while the powerful secure eternal impunity through mutual endorsement.
