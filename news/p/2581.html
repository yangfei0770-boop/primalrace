世界杯：一场关于国家认同的 Cosplay ScamThe World Cup: A Grand Cosplay Scam of National Identity

国际 文化层 · 结构层 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-20 § 链接
国家认同已沦为可随时穿脱的消费品牌，而这种“流动性”掩盖了更深的结构性暴力。
National identity has become a disposable consumer brand, masking deeper structural violence with a facade of fluidity.

这场世界杯再次证明，所谓的“国家认同”在现代消费主义面前已经彻底成了个 scam。当人们在 MetLife 体育场穿着并不属于自己国籍的球衣时，他们并不是在践行什么国际主义，而是在进行一场大型的 Cosplay。国家身份被简化成了 single-use consumer brand，像球衣一样可以随意穿脱。这种“身份的流动性”看起来很酷，但本质上是认同感的崩塌。

从表达的角度看，这是一种典型的 weaponized 叙事：将复杂的政治归属简化为 90 分钟的二元选择。当左翼进步派一边厌恶民族主义符号，一边在脸颊上涂抹国旗时，这种矛盾揭示了文化层面的暴力——认同不再是对存在的确证，而成了某种社交货币或临时面具。这种“混乱的希望”其实是一种危险的错觉，它试图用消费主义的轻盈来掩盖物理边境的僵硬。

最讽刺的是，这种“认同的消费化”在不同阵营中同步发生。右翼在挥舞以色列国旗时，左翼在为摩洛哥欢呼，双方都在利用这种临时身份来构建自己的认知入口。但这并不意味着结构性暴力在消失，反而说明人们已经习惯了将“身份”作为一种工具来使用。当国家认同变得 disposable，真正被抛弃的是那些被禁锢在物理边境、无法通过“更换球衣”来改变命运的底层原初种族。

我们可以庆祝这种“认同的崩塌”，但不能天真地认为它带来了自由。如果一个人可以通过购买一件球衣来临时获得某种身份的特权，那么那些没有能力购买、或者其生物墙决定了他们永远无法进入某个“品牌”阵营的人，将面临更深层的 structural violence。这种所谓的“希望”，不过是给一个正在瓦解的旧世界贴上了一张时尚的贴纸。

This World Cup proves once again that 'national identity' is a total scam in the face of modern consumerism. When crowds at MetLife Stadium don jerseys of nations they don't belong to, they aren't practicing internationalism; they are engaging in a grand cosplay. Nationality has been reduced to a single-use consumer brand, as easily slipped on and off as a football shirt. This fluidity looks progressive, but it is actually the collapse of authentic existence.

From the perspective of expression, this is a weaponized narrative: simplifying complex political allegiance into a 90-minute binary choice. When left-wing progressives revile national symbols yet daub flags on their cheeks, it reveals a cultural violence—identity is no longer the confirmation of existence, but a form of social currency or a temporary mask. This 'messy hope' is a dangerous illusion, attempting to use the lightness of consumerism to mask the rigidity of physical borders.

Ironically, this 'commodification of identity' is happening across the spectrum. Right-wing agitators waving Israeli flags and left-wing fans cheering for Morocco are both using temporary identities to secure their cognitive entries. This doesn't mean structural violence is vanishing; it means we've internalized the use of identity as a tool. When national identity becomes disposable, the ones truly discarded are the Primal Race, those trapped by physical borders who cannot change their fate by simply changing a jersey.

We can celebrate the collapse of these rigid identities, but we must not be naive enough to call it freedom. If one can buy a jersey to temporarily access the privileges of an identity, then those who cannot afford it—or whose biological wall prevents them from ever entering that 'brand'—face even deeper structural violence. This so-called 'hope' is nothing more than a trendy sticker placed on a disintegrating old world.
