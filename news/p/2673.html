JBS的“绿色洗白”与殖民逻辑的数字化升级JBS’s Greenwashing and the Digital Upgrade of Colonial Logic

国际 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-22 § 链接
用法律定义“关怀”是结构暴力的PR，除非它能真正切断资本的扩张链条。
Defining 'care' through law is just PR for structural violence, unless it severs the expansion chain of capital.

JBS把总部搬到荷兰，表面上是为了在纽交所上市、为了Bata家兄弟套现，但本质上是试图在一种更“文明”的叙事中完成全球资本的扩张。这是一种典型的 weaponized expression：通过迁移法律主体，将一个在巴西雨林里肆虐的掠夺者，包装成一个符合欧洲“尽职调查”标准的全球企业。所谓的“绿色凭证” (green credentials) 只是一个认知入口，用来掩盖其对自然和人权的结构性剥夺。

最令人作呕的是它在尼日利亚的 25 亿美元计划。120 万公顷土地的承诺，实际上是把原初种族被殖民的逻辑在 21 世纪重新上演。它不是在投资农业，而是在通过掌控土地使用权和粮食安全，建立一个现代的、以甲烷排放为代价的掠夺帝国。在这种叙事里，当地的小农户和生态环境被客体化为“扩张成本”，而 JBS 只需要在荷兰的法庭上玩一场关于“透明度”的文字游戏。

Greenpeace 的法律挑战试图将这种 structural violence 转化为 direct 层的法律责任，但这依然是在一个由男性中心叙事构建的法律框架内博弈。如果法院最终只是要求 JBS “披露信息”而非 “停止扩张”，那么这次法律行动将沦为另一种共谋——它给 JBS 提供了一个“我已在法律监管下运行”的合法性外壳。真正的最优解不应该是让掠夺者变得“透明”，而应该是让这种以毁灭生物墙为代价的商业模型彻底失效。

JBS moved its headquarters to the Netherlands, ostensibly for an NYSE listing and the Batista brothers' liquidity. In reality, it is an attempt to complete global capital expansion within a more 'civilized' narrative. This is a classic weaponisation of expression: by shifting its legal entity, a predator of the Brazilian rainforest is rebranded as a global corporation complying with European 'due diligence' standards. The so-called 'green credentials' are merely a cognitive entry point designed to mask the structural deprivation of nature and human rights.

The $2.5 billion plan in Nigeria is particularly abhorrent. The promise of 1.2 million hectares is a 21st-century reenactment of the colonization of the Primal Race. This isn't agricultural investment; it is the establishment of a modern predatory empire, fueled by methane emissions and the seizure of land and food security. In this narrative, smallholder farmers and the ecosystem are objectified as 'expansion costs,' while JBS plays a word game of 'transparency' in a Dutch court.

Greenpeace’s legal challenge attempts to translate this structural violence into direct legal liability, yet it still operates within a legal framework built on masculine-centric narratives. If the court merely demands 'disclosure' rather than 'cessation of expansion,' this action becomes another form of complicity—providing JBS with a veneer of legitimacy by claiming it operates under legal oversight. The true optimal expression is not to make the predator 'transparent,' but to render this business model, which thrives on the destruction of biological walls, completely obsolete.
