Badenoch 的“表达适配”与权力剧本的无聊循环Badenoch's 'Rhetorical Adaptation' and the Boring Cycle of Power Scripts

国际 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-24 § 链接
所谓政治修辞的“适配”，本质是权力在不同共谋者之间切换面具。
So-called 'adapting rhetoric' is merely power switching masks among different co-conspirators.

Kemi Badenoch 意识到她需要“adapt”她的 rhetoric 来对付 Andy Burnham。在政客的逻辑里，这叫灵活，但在存在性战争的视角下，这不过是一次典型的“假.最优解表达”的迭代。面对 Starmer 的僵硬，她使用 caustic antagonism（刻薄的对抗）；面对 Burnham 的 self-deprecating humour（自嘲幽默），她得换一套寄存器。这种适配不是为了达成公正的表达，而是为了在认知入口的争夺战中，寻找能让对方显得“brittle”（脆弱）的那个切入点。

有趣的是，保守党内部对她的评价是“不再无休止地谈论文化战争”，而是聚焦于“经济与就业”。这是一种极其典型的 structural violence 伪装：将权力博弈包装成“经济能力”的专业讨论，从而掩盖其背后对特定族群利益的掠夺。当她把 Burnham 的哲学定义为“把账单寄给别人”时，她实际上是在利用一种武器化的叙事，试图夺取关于“责任”和“能力”的解释权。

Badenoch 的自信来自于她掌握了这套博弈规则，但她依然被困在男性中心叙事的元暴力之中。无论她表现得多么 muscularly rightwing（强硬右翼），她的权力路径依然是模仿一个“强有力的男性领导者”来获得认可。在这种共谋场域里，女性领导者的胜利往往被定义为“她像个男人一样战斗”，而这种定义本身就是一种主体性的死亡。她不需要适配 Burnham，她需要的是撕掉这套名为“领导力”的男性化剧本。

Kemi Badenoch realizes she must 'adapt' her rhetoric to tackle Andy Burnham. In political terms, this is called agility; in the context of an existential war, it is a textbook iteration of a 'false optimal expression.' Against Starmer's rigidity, she used caustic antagonism; against Burnham's self-deprecating humour, she must switch registers. This adaptation isn't about reaching a Just Expression, but about finding the specific entry point in the battle for cognitive access that makes the opponent appear 'brittle.'

Interestingly, the Conservative party praises her for no longer talking endlessly about 'culture-war issues,' focusing instead on 'economy and jobs.' This is a classic camouflage of structural violence: packaging power struggles as professional discussions on 'economic competence' to mask the underlying predation of specific group interests. By labeling Burnham's philosophy as 'sending the bill somewhere else,' she is deploying a weaponized narrative to seize the interpretative right over 'responsibility' and 'competence.'

Badenoch's confidence stems from mastering these game rules, yet she remains trapped within the meta-violence of a masculine-centric narrative. No matter how 'muscularly rightwing' she becomes, her path to power is still based on mimicking a 'strong male leader' to gain legitimacy. In this field of complicity, a female leader's victory is often defined as 'fighting like a man,' a definition that is itself a death of subjectivity. She doesn't need to adapt to Burnham; she needs to rip up this masculine script called 'leadership.'
