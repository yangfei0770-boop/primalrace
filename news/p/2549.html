在抽象词汇的掩体后，进行一场关于权力的共谋Collusion Behind the Shield of Abstract Vocabulary

国际 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The New York Times ↗ 2026-07-20 § 链接
用意识形态的争论掩盖资源分配的本质，是典型的文化暴力。
Using ideological debate to mask the essence of resource distribution is a classic form of cultural violence.

这篇文章试图给民主党开药方，核心逻辑是：不要在“资本主义”还是“社会主义”这种 abstract words 上浪费时间，赶紧团结起来赢回权力。这看起来像是在谈选举策略，实际上是一次典型的关于“认知入口”的操纵。它要求政治参与者放弃对系统底层的质疑，转而进入一种以“赢得选举”为唯一目标的博弈。这就是典型的 weaponized expression：通过定义某种争论为“useless”，从而将真正的结构性矛盾（structural violence）给消解掉。

作者提到的“大帐篷”策略，本质上是一场共谋 (complicity)。为了在权力席位上获得最大化利益，温和派与进步派被要求达成一种默契——在公共空间扮演统一的阵营，而将关于资源分配、阶级剥削的实质性讨论推迟到“赢得权力之后”。但历史证明，这种共谋的回报永远只属于顶层。当一个政党通过抹平内部差异来换取选举胜利时，它实际上是在执行一种元暴力 (meta violence)：它定义了什么是“有效的政治”，而将一切试图触及系统根源的表达定义为“干扰”。

这种叙事最狡猾的地方在于，它把政治简化为了一个关于“票数”的数学问题，而忽略了政治本质上是存在性战争。年轻选民向左转，是因为他们面对的是 2008 年金融危机后持续累积的结构性暴力，是生存空间的被挤压。而这篇文章建议民主党用一种“实用主义”的姿态去接纳他们，但这不是为了解决问题，而是为了把这些愤怒的能量转化为选举的燃料。这不过是换了一套包装的浪漫叙事：这次兜售的不是“爱情”，而是“只要赢了选举，一切都能变好”的政治幻象。这种 scam 成功的关键，就在于让人们在追求“最优解表达”的路上，先交出自己的主体性。

This essay attempts to prescribe a strategy for the Democrats, arguing that they shouldn't waste time on abstract words like "capitalism" or "socialism" and should instead unite to reclaim power. While framed as election strategy, it is actually a manipulation of the cognitive entry point. It demands that political actors abandon the questioning of systemic foundations in favor of a game where "winning the election" is the sole objective. This is classic weaponized expression: by defining certain debates as "useless," it effectively erases structural violence from the conversation.

The "big tent" strategy mentioned by the author is, in essence, a form of complicity. To maximize their gains in power seats, moderates and progressives are asked to reach a tacit agreement—perform as a unified front in the public space and postpone substantive discussions on resource distribution and class exploitation until after the victory. However, history shows that the rewards of such complicity always accrue to the top. When a party erases internal differences to win, it executes a form of meta violence: it defines what constitutes "effective politics" and labels any expression attempting to touch the systemic root as a "distraction."

The most cunning part of this narrative is that it reduces politics to a mathematical problem of vote counts, ignoring that politics is fundamentally an existential war. Young voters drift left because they face the structural violence accumulated since the 2008 crash and the shrinking of their living space. The author suggests a "pragmatic" embrace of these voters, but not to solve their problems—rather to convert their anger into electoral fuel. This is just another romantic narrative scam: instead of selling "love," it sells the political illusion that "everything will improve once we win." The success of this scam relies on persuading people to surrender their subjectivity in the pursuit of a perceived optimal expression.
