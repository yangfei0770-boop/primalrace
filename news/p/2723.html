AI泡沫：一场关于“未来利润”的认知收割The AI Bubble: A Cognitive Harvest of 'Future Profits'

科技 结构层 · 文化层 The New York Times ↗ 2026-07-22 § 链接
当经济增长依赖于对未来的虚构，这种增长本身就是一种结构性暴力。
When economic growth relies on a fiction of the future, that growth is itself a form of structural violence.

金融界有个老梗：股市不等于经济。但现在，这个谎言被撕开了。美国股市市值是GDP的2.5倍，其中巨大的差额不是由生产力填补的，而是由一个名为“AI”的叙事支撑的。这本质上是一场大规模的 weaponized expression：通过制造一个关于“无限可能性”的认知入口，将未来的预期利润提前贴现到今天，从而在纸面上创造出天文数字的财富。

这种财富通过 affluent Americans 的消费行为（奢侈度假、高端餐饮）转化为实体的经济活动，看起来在支撑经济，实际上是在通过一种极其危险的共谋来维持现状。资本市场、技术巨头与消费阶层共同构建了一个“AI故事”，在这个故事里，只要信心不崩塌，所有人都能在泡沫中分一杯羹。但这是一种典型的假.最优解表达——它不产生真正的系统性公正，而是在用一个不可持续的幻象掩盖结构性的脆弱。

最讽刺的是，这种“繁荣”其实是另一种形式的 structural violence。当整个经济体被绑定在一个单一的叙事节点上时，底层与中产的生存状态实际上被交给了几个科技巨头的股价波动。如果 AI 泡沫破裂，被牺牲的绝不是那些在山顶套现的精英，而是那些在基础设施建设中被异化为耗材的劳动力，以及在消费主义陷阱中被透支的普通人。

这不过是另一个版本的“钻石恒久远”或“996是福报”：用一个被神化的概念，诱导人们在潜意识里接受一种极不平等的资源分配方式。当一个国家的经济增长需要靠“讲故事”来维持时，这个故事本身就是最强大的武器，而我们所有人，都是这场认知博弈中的筹码。

There is an old financial adage: the stock market is not the economy. But now, the lie is exposed. With the U.S. stock market value at 2.5 times the annual GDP, the massive gap isn't filled by productivity, but by a narrative called "AI." This is essentially a large-scale weaponized expression: by manufacturing a cognitive entry point of "boundless possibility," future expected profits are discounted into today's value, creating astronomical wealth on paper.

This wealth translates into real economic activity through the spending of affluent Americans—luxury vacations and high-end dining. It looks like economic support, but it is actually a dangerous complicity maintaining the status quo. The capital markets, tech giants, and the consumer class have collectively constructed an "AI story." As long as confidence doesn't falter, everyone gets a piece of the bubble. However, this is a fake optimal expression; it creates no systemic justice, only a fragile existence built on an unsustainable illusion.

Most ironically, this "boom" is another form of structural violence. When an entire economy is tethered to a single narrative node, the survival of the bottom and middle classes is effectively handed over to the stock fluctuations of a few tech giants. If the AI bubble bursts, those sacrificed won't be the elites who cashed out at the peak, but the laborers alienated as consumables in infrastructure projects and the ordinary people exhausted by consumerist traps.

This is just another version of "A Diamond is Forever" or "996 is a blessing": using a fetishized concept to induce people into accepting an extremely unequal distribution of resources. When a nation's growth depends on "storytelling," the story itself becomes the most powerful weapon, and we are all merely chips in this existential game of cognitive博弈.
