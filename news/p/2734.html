核协议与导弹雨：一场关于“定义权”的资源围猎Nuclear Deals and Missile Rain: A Resource Hunt for the Right to Define

国际 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-23 § 链接
战争叙事中的“文明”与“安全”，本质上是权力对资源定价权的暴力抢夺。
The narrative of 'civilization' and 'security' is essentially a violent seizure of the power to price resources.

美国在对伊朗实施第12个夜晚的导弹轰炸时，顺手给沙特签了一份核能协议。这种极其荒诞的同步性，撕开了所谓“维护航行自由”和“防止核扩散”的文化伪装。逻辑很简单：核扩散是禁忌，但只要你是我的共谋者，这个禁忌就可以被定义为“和平核能合作”。

这不仅是地缘博弈，而是一次典型的表达武器化。美国通过定义谁是“威胁”，从而合法化对伊朗的直接暴力；同时通过定义谁是“合作伙伴”，将核技术这种顶级资源作为筹码，在结构层重新分配权力。在这种叙事中，伊朗的核计划是“恐怖”，而沙特的核计划是“协议”。这种对同一事实的不同定义，就是元暴力的运作方式——解释权被垄断在强者手中。

至于胡塞武装在红海的封锁和油价的上涨，不过是这场存在性战争的副产品。全球消费者的通胀压力，本质上是男性中心叙事下，几个大国男性领导者在进行一场关于“谁能掌控全球能源咽喉”的雄性竞技。他们将地缘政治简化为一种“眼还眼”的权力博弈，而真正承受结构性暴力的，是那些在边境袭击中死亡的平民，以及被推向战争前线的肉身。

这场危机的本质不是什么“危机”，而是一次精准的资源围猎。所谓的“和平”与“安全”，永远是为共谋者准备的入场券。

As the US launches its 12th consecutive night of missile strikes on Iran, it simultaneously signs a nuclear deal with Saudi Arabia. This absurd synchronicity strips away the cultural mask of 'maintaining freedom of navigation' and 'preventing nuclear proliferation.' The logic is simple: nuclear proliferation is a taboo, but as long as you are a complicity partner, that taboo is redefined as 'peaceful nuclear cooperation.'

This is not merely geopolitical maneuvering; it is a classic weaponisation of expression. By defining who constitutes a 'threat,' the US legitimizes direct violence against Iran. Simultaneously, by defining who is a 'partner,' it uses nuclear technology—a top-tier resource—as a chip to redistribute power at the structural level. In this narrative, Iran's nuclear program is 'terror,' while Saudi Arabia's is an 'agreement.' This disparate definition of the same fact is exactly how meta-violence operates—the monopoly of interpretation remains with the powerful.

As for the Houthi blockade in the Red Sea and the subsequent spike in oil prices, these are merely the by-products of this existential war. The inflation felt by global consumers is, in essence, a masculine-centric narrative where male leaders of superpowers engage in a testosterone-driven competition over who controls the global energy chokeholds. They reduce geopolitics to an 'eye for an eye' power game, while the structural violence is borne by civilians killed in border strikes and the physical bodies pushed to the front lines.

The essence of this crisis is not a 'crisis' at all, but a precise resource hunt. So-called 'peace' and 'security' are always just entry tickets reserved for the co-conspirators.
