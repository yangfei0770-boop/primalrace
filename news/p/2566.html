洗发水的仪式感，是一场关于“洁净”的商业scamThe Shampoo Ritual: A Commercial Scam of 'Cleanliness'

哲学 文化层 · 结构层 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-20 § 链接
所谓的“护理习惯”往往是商业武器化叙事对生物本能的成功殖民。
So-called 'care habits' are often successful colonizations of biological instincts by weaponized commercial narratives.

这篇文章描述的不是一个简单的护发心得，而是一个个体在意识到自己被 commercial scam 操控后，试图夺回生物性表达权的微小博弈。洗发水在现代文明中被包装成像刷牙一样不可或缺的 ritual，但这本质上是一次认知入口的劫持：商业公司定义了什么是“干净”，然后制造出一种“不使用产品=肮脏”的文化焦虑，迫使消费者陷入“洗涤-干枯-使用护发素-更依赖洗涤”的结构性循环。

这种对身体的规训具有极强的惯性。作者提到在前两周里，他会下意识地伸手去拿洗发水，这正是内化机制的典型表现——即使身体在抗议，大脑中的“社会化程序”依然在强制执行。这种对生物本能的覆盖，让人们在追求“标准洁净”的过程中，实际上在削弱自身的生物墙，让头皮和发质在化学试剂的反复轰炸下丧失自然平衡。

好在这次博弈的结果是作者找到了他的 真.最优解表达。当他通过物理上的“不作为”，拆穿了商业叙事制造的伪需求，他的身体才重新获得了定义“健康”的解释权。这不仅仅是头发变亮的问题，而是一个人意识到，那些被标榜为“文明”和“科学”的日常习惯，往往是资本为了维持利润而投放的武器。

This piece is not merely a haircare tip, but a micro-game of an individual attempting to reclaim their biological expression after realizing they were played by a commercial scam. In modern civilization, shampoo has been packaged as a ritual as essential as brushing teeth. This is essentially a hijacking of the cognitive entry point: corporations define 'cleanliness,' then manufacture a cultural anxiety that 'no product = dirty,' forcing consumers into a structural loop of 'strip-dry-condition-depend.'

The inertia of this bodily discipline is profound. The author's instinctive reach for the bottle during the first two weeks is a classic manifestation of internalisation—where the 'socialized program' in the brain overrides the body's own protests. This erasure of biological instinct, in the pursuit of a 'standard clean,' actually weakens the biological wall, leaving the scalp and hair stripped of natural balance by chemical agents.

Fortunately, the result of this game is the author finding his True Optimal Expression. By physically 'doing nothing,' he dismantled the pseudo-demand created by the commercial narrative, allowing his body to regain the power to define 'health.' It is not just about shinier hair; it is about realizing that the daily habits lauded as 'civilized' or 'scientific' are often weapons deployed by capital to sustain profit.
