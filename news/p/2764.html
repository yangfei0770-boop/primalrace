以“眼还眼”为名的能源勒索与宏大叙事的代价Energy Blackmail Under the Guise of 'An Eye for an Eye'

国际 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-23 § 链接
所谓的“地缘政治博弈”本质上是男性权力机器在用全球底层的生存成本买单。
Geopolitical gambling is simply masculine power machines spending the survival costs of the global underclass.

又是典型的“男性中心叙事” (masculine-centric narrative) 在操盘。胡塞武装用“以眼还眼”的 Equation 包装勒索，美国和伊朗在 ceasefire 的博弈中把海峡当筹码，沙特在管道和航道之间做风险对冲。在这个巨大的博弈场里，油价突破 100 美元被描述成一种“市场波动”或“地缘危机”，但实际上这是一场极其典型的结构性暴力 (structural violence)。

注意这个逻辑：权力上位者通过掌控认知入口，将这种冲突定义为“战略对抗”或“正义之战”，从而掩盖了其本质——即通过制造稀缺来夺取定价权。当油价飙升，承受代价的绝不是那些在视频声明里叫嚣的军事发言人，而是那些在 fiscal room 极小的贫困国家里，因为运输成本增加而面临饥荒的女性和儿童。这种从海峡封锁到餐桌物价的传导，就是 Violence = Potential − Actual 的具体体现：人们本可以拥有稳定的能源供应，但因为几个男权政权的存在性战争，这个差额变成了具体的饥饿与贫困。

最讽刺的是，这种“大棋局”的叙事逻辑在文化层 (cultural layer) 被美化为一种强者的博弈艺术。无论是所谓的“国际法”还是“海盗行径”，都不过是男性权力机器在不同阶段切换的武器化表达。他们通过定义什么是“合法”的封锁，来掩盖其对全球资源分配权的粗暴垄断。在这种元暴力的掩盖下，全球能源依赖成了一个巨大的 scam，而底层民众则成了这场博弈中被视作可消耗的、无名的零件。

Another textbook case of masculine-centric narrative in play. The Houthis wrap their extortion in an 'eye for an eye' equation, the US and Iran treat straits as bargaining chips in their ceasefire game, and Saudi Arabia hedges risks between pipelines and shipping lanes. In this massive arena, oil breaching $100 is described as 'market volatility' or a 'geopolitical crisis,' but it is, in fact, pure structural violence.

Observe the logic: those at the top of the power hierarchy use their control over cognitive entry points to define this conflict as 'strategic confrontation' or a 'war of justice,' thereby masking the essence—the seizure of pricing power through manufactured scarcity. When oil prices surge, the cost is not borne by military spokespeople shouting in video statements, but by women and children in countries with minimal fiscal room, facing famine as transport costs rise. This transmission from strait blockades to dinner table prices is the concrete manifestation of Violence = Potential − Actual: the gap between a stable energy supply and the current reality is the direct result of existential wars between patriarchal regimes.

Most ironic is how this 'grand chessboard' logic is glamorized in the cultural layer as the art of strongman gambling. Whether it's 'international law' or 'acts of piracy,' these are merely weaponized expressions switched by masculine power machines at different stages. By defining what constitutes a 'legal' blockade, they mask their brutal monopoly over global resource distribution. Under the cover of this meta-violence, global fossil fuel dependence has become a massive scam, while the global underclass are treated as disposable, nameless components in the machine.
