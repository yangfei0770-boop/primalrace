Jessica Morgan 的“薪资折扣”与主体性陷阱Jessica Morgan’s ‘Pay Cut’ and the Trap of Subjectivity

性别 结构层 · 文化层 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-22 § 链接
用薪资折损换取的权力席位，本质上是结构性共谋的PR版本。
Power seats acquired through salary cuts are merely PR versions of structural complicity.

Jessica Morgan 回到 Tate 担任馆长，被冠以“变革代理人” (agent of change) 的光环，但新闻中最值得玩味的是那个被反复强调的 “big pay cut”。在权力博弈中，薪资不仅是经济报酬，更是对该席位定价权的确认。当一名女性领导者被要求接受大幅减薪才能进入一个核心权力席位时，这绝不是什么“情怀回归”，而是一次典型的 structural violence：系统在告诉你，这个位置的价值在男性中心叙事下是固定的，而女性的进入需要通过自我折损来抵消其带来的“扰动”。

Tate 的主席 Roland Rudd 强调她能“带着人们一起走” (carried people with her)，这实际上是对女性领导者的一种潜意识规训——你必须在展现能力的同时，维持一个不具攻击性、温顺且能达成共识的形象，才能被这个由男性定义规则的委员会接纳。这种“最优解表达”是危险的，因为它要求女性在获取权力时，必须先通过扮演一个“体面且不贪婪”的角色来换取入场券。主体性的确立不应该建立在对自身定价权的自我阉割之上。

至于她被描述为“最酷的馆长”以及对设计师服装的偏好，这不过是文化层面的掩护。当权力结构依然要求她接受 pay cut 时，她的审美表达就成了一种被允许的、无害的装饰，用来稀释她作为权力挑战者的锋芒。一个真正的变革者不应该在入职前就与既定秩序达成关于“低价买入”的共谋。

Jessica Morgan’s return to Tate as director, framed as an “agent of change,” is overshadowed by the recurring mention of a “big pay cut.” In the game of existence, salary is not just compensation; it is the confirmation of the seat's pricing power. When a female leader is expected to accept a significant pay cut to occupy a core power position, it is not a “labor of love,” but a clear manifestation of structural violence. The system signals that the value of this role is fixed under a masculine-centric narrative, and a woman’s entry must be offset by self-depreciation to minimize the “disruption” she brings.

Tate’s chair, Roland Rudd, praises her ability to “carry people with her.” This is a subconscious discipline for female leaders: you must remain non-aggressive and consensus-driven to be accepted by a committee that defines the rules. This “optimal expression” is a trap; it requires women to play the role of the “gracious and ungreedy” candidate to secure their ticket. The establishment of subjectivity should never be predicated on the self-castration of one’s own pricing power.

Her description as the “coolest director” and her taste in designers are merely cultural smokescreens. When the structural reality demands a pay cut, her aesthetic expression becomes a permitted, harmless ornament used to dilute her edge as a challenger of power. A true agent of change should not enter a role by forming a complicity with the existing order over a “discounted” price.
