味觉逻辑的跨国共谋：被掩盖的殖民底色Transnational Complicity of Taste: The Masked Colonial Subtext

哲学 结构层 · 文化层 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-24 § 链接
相似的味觉逻辑不是巧合，而是被殖民历史修剪后的共谋。
Similar flavor logics are not coincidences, but complicities pruned by colonial history.

一个马来西亚人在墨西哥吃冰棍，感叹两种文化在“甜味、盐、辣椒、酸味”上的逻辑共振。这种叙事在《卫报》这种体面的媒体看来是某种温情的、跨国界的文化共情，但在我的眼睛里，这不过是两组被殖民历史修剪过的生物样本在进行一次迟到的认亲。

这种所谓的“口味逻辑” (flavour logic) 其实是典型的 structural violence 留下的生物印记。马来西亚和墨西哥，一个被英国殖民，一个被西班牙殖民。殖民者不仅掠夺了资源，更通过对农业种植的强行干预，决定了这些土地上哪些作物能生存，哪些口味被定义为“异域”或“底层”。当作者在感叹芒果和菠萝的“明亮”与“生动”时，她其实是在消费一种被过滤掉痛苦的殖民残余。

这就是一种 cultural violence 的高级版本：将生存的匮乏和被殖民的痕迹，包装成一种“多元且迷人”的审美趣味 (Taste)。这种叙事把复杂的历史权力关系简化为一种厨房里的 recipe，让读者在享受这种“异域风情”时，完全忘记了这种味觉共识的底层逻辑是：他们都被同一个名为“帝国”的机器处理成了相似的客体。

好新闻应该是揭露这种共谋，而不是在一个甜点食谱里通过感叹“感觉很熟悉”来完成一次廉价的文化和解。这种“熟悉感”不是桥梁，而是伤疤的共振。

A Malaysian encounters paletas in Mexico and marvels at the shared 'flavour logic' of sweetness, salt, and chilli. To The Guardian, this is a heartwarming tale of cross-cultural empathy. To me, it is merely two biological samples, both pruned by colonial history, recognizing each other in a delayed reunion.

This 'flavour logic' is a textbook imprint of structural violence. Malaysia under the British, Mexico under the Spanish. Colonizers did not just steal resources; they dictated which crops survived and which tastes were branded as 'exotic' or 'primitive' through forced agricultural intervention. While the author celebrates the 'brightness' of mango and pineapple, she is consuming a filtered residue of colonialism, stripped of its agony.

This is a sophisticated version of cultural violence: packaging the scars of deprivation and colonial subjugation as a 'diverse and charming' aesthetic Taste. This narrative reduces complex power dynamics to a kitchen recipe, allowing the reader to enjoy 'exoticism' while forgetting that this taste consensus was forged by a single machine called 'Empire' that processed both populations into similar objects.

Good news should expose this complicity, not achieve a cheap cultural reconciliation by claiming a 'strange familiarity' in a dessert column. This familiarity is not a bridge; it is the resonance of shared scars.
