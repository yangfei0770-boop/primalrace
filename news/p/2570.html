用“人民”做掩体，是权力共谋的经典scamUsing 'The People' as a Shield: The Classic Scam of Power Complicity

国际 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-20 § 链接
将制度审查定义为“政治迫害”，是特权阶层夺取解释权的常规武器化叙事。
Defining systemic scrutiny as 'political persecution' is a standard weaponized narrative for elites to seize the power of interpretation.

Nigel Farage 和他的追随者在玩一场极其熟练的表达博弈。当一个涉嫌隐瞒500万英镑巨额赠款的政治人物面对标准审查时，他不去讨论规则的公正性，而是迅速将认知入口转向“Kangaroo Court”（袋鼠法庭）。这是一个典型的武器化叙事：通过定义审查机制为“非法”和“政治工具”，他成功地将一个关于诚信与法律的 Structural 议题，置换成了关于“精英阶层 vs 平民意志”的文化战争。

这种叙事最阴险的地方在于，它利用了结构性弱势者对制度的不信任感。Sarah Pochin 所谓的“将权力交还给人民”，本质上是一次表演性的让步。在 Clacton 的补选中，他把选票当成洗白私德的遮羞布，试图用一个简单的“支持/不支持”投票，来覆盖掉他与商人、诈骗犯之间复杂的利益共谋。这不过是另一种形式的 Meta Violence——用一种伪装成“民主”的表达，去消灭对特权进行审计的制度可能性。

Farage 提到的那个“顶级国际律师”的建议，正是典型的共谋者逻辑：只要能找到一套能够自圆其说的法律解释，就可以将掠夺或不透明的利益交换定义为“无条件赠予”。在男本位的权力结构中，这种对规则的随意解读和对解释权的垄断，正是元暴力的具体体现。他们不关心事实是否真实，只关心如何制造一个对自身有利的“真实”。

这场博弈的结局很讽刺：主流政党通过抵制补选来维持某种体面，而 Farage 则在自己的回音室里通过扮演“受害者”来增强存在感。在这种共谋场域中，真正被牺牲掉的是公众对政治透明度的认知。当“违反规则”被包装成“反抗体制”的勋章时，公正的表达已经彻底死亡。

Nigel Farage and his followers are playing a sophisticated game of expression. When a politician suspected of concealing a £5m gift faces a standards probe, he doesn't argue the fairness of the rules; instead, he swiftly shifts the cognitive entry point to the narrative of a 'Kangaroo Court.' This is a classic weaponization of expression: by defining the scrutiny mechanism as 'illegitimate' and a 'political tool,' he transforms a structural issue of integrity and law into a cultural war between 'the elite' and 'the people.'

The most insidious part of this narrative is its exploitation of the structural disadvantaged's distrust in institutions. Sarah Pochin's claim of 'handing power back to the people' is a performative concession. In the Clacton by-election, Farage uses the ballot box as a shroud for his private misconduct, attempting to overwrite the complex complicity between himself, businessmen, and fraudsters with a binary 'support/oppose' vote. This is another form of Meta Violence—using a narrative disguised as 'democracy' to erase the possibility of systemic auditing of privilege.

Farage's reference to a 'top international lawyer' is the textbook logic of the co-conspirator: as long as a legal interpretation can be constructed to justify the act, predatory or opaque interest exchanges can be rebranded as 'unconditional gifts.' In a masculine-centric power structure, this arbitrary manipulation of rules and monopoly over interpretation is the embodiment of Meta Violence. They do not care if the facts are true; they only care about manufacturing a 'truth' that serves their interests.

The irony of this game is that while mainstream parties maintain a facade of decency by boycotting the contest, Farage strengthens his presence by playing the 'victim' within his own echo chamber. In this field of complicity, the real casualty is the public's perception of political transparency. When 'breaking the rules' is rebranded as a badge of 'defying the system,' Just Expression is effectively dead.
