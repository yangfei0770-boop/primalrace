被制度性遗忘的受害者与被法律赦免的共谋Institutional Oblivion and the Complicity of Legal Absolution

哲学 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-22 § 链接
Corporate manslaughter 是结构暴力的 PR 掩体，而非正义的抵达。
Corporate manslaughter is a PR shield for structural violence, not the arrival of justice.

当警方开始调查 NHS trust 的 "corporate manslaughter" 时，公众习惯性地将其视为一种问责的胜利。但根据加尔通的暴力三角，这不过是在 direct 层面上试图给 structural violence 贴一个刑事标签。真正的暴力不在于一个精神分裂症患者的失控，而在于一个系统如何通过 "找不到他" 这种极低成本的行政理由，将一个已知具有杀人潜能的个体从监管网中剔除，从而将潜在的血案转化为一种 "不可抗力" 的意外。

这是一场典型的共谋。NHS trust、当地警察局以及监管机构，在各自的岗位上通过 "未能执行逮捕令" 或 "无法联系患者" 这种形式的失职，共同构建了一个巨大的真空地带。在这个真空里，医疗资源的分配不均和管理层对风险的漠视被包装成 "医疗失误"。当一个医生明确警告该患者可能杀人，而系统却在两年内四次将其收容又将其放逐，这种循环本身就是一种元暴力：它定义了谁的生命是可被放弃的，以及什么样的管理失败是可以被容忍的。

所谓的 "corporate manslaughter" 调查，其结果大概率是巨额罚款和几名高管的离职。这种赔偿机制本质上是将人的生命在结构层面上进行了 "定价"。它并没有缩小 Potential 与 Actual 的差额，而是通过一次表演性的法律程序，让共谋者们在支付了某种 "违约金" 后，继续维持这套低效且残酷的医疗叙事。只要这种 "制度性失职" 依然被视为管理漏洞而非蓄意剥夺，下一个受害者只是时间问题。

When police investigate an NHS trust for "corporate manslaughter," the public tends to see it as a victory for accountability. However, applying Galtung's Violence Triangle, this is merely an attempt to slap a criminal label on structural violence at the direct level. The real violence isn't the breakdown of a schizophrenic patient, but how a system used the low-cost administrative excuse of "couldn't find him" to excise a known high-risk individual from the regulatory net, transforming a predictable massacre into an "unforeseeable" accident.

This is a textbook case of complicity. The NHS trust, local police, and regulatory bodies collaborated through "failures to act on warrants" and "inability to contact patients," collectively constructing a void of accountability. In this void, the inequity of healthcare resource allocation and managerial indifference are rebranded as "medical errors." When a doctor explicitly warns that a patient could kill, yet the system admits and discharges him four times in two years, the cycle itself becomes meta-violence: it defines whose life is expendable and what level of systemic failure is tolerable.

The investigation into "corporate manslaughter" will likely culminate in substantial fines and a few leadership changes. This compensatory mechanism essentially "prices" human life at the structural level. It does not narrow the gap between Potential and Actual; instead, it allows the co-conspirators to maintain a cruel and inefficient healthcare narrative after paying a form of "breach of contract" fee. As long as "institutional failure" is treated as a loophole rather than a deliberate deprivation, the next victim is merely a matter of time.
