用“污水”淹没事实：一种极其低端的认知武器化Drowning Truth in Sewage: A Low-End Art of Weaponized Expression

国际 文化层 · 结构层 · 元暴力 The New York Times ↗ 2026-07-23 § 链接
当真相被定义为“污水”时，权力就不再需要逻辑，只需要共谋。
When truth is defined as 'sewage', power no longer requires logic, only complicity.

这篇文章试图分析特朗普如何通过“妖魔化”和“制造阴谋论”来操纵选举，但它陷入了典型的自由派陷阱：把这看作是一个政治人物的个人诡计，而非一套成熟的表达武器化 (Weaponisation of Expression) 流程。

特朗普的操作路径极其标准：首先通过宗教和恐惧制造认知入口，将对手定义为“非人”的威胁；随后通过“ flooding the media with sewage”——即用海量、低质、矛盾的虚假信息淹没公共讨论空间。这不是在争论事实，而是在摧毁“事实”这个概念本身。当一个人不再关心什么是真的，而只关心谁在为自己的阵营说话时，解释权就彻底从理性逻辑转移到了身份认同的博弈中。

更深层的恐怖在于 structural violence 的介入。作者提到的 1887 年《选举计票法》被滥用的可能，证明了文化层面的“污水”只是前戏，最终目的是为了在制度层面上完成对权力的非法攫取。这是一种典型的元暴力 (Meta Violence) 运作：先用叙事洗脑，让支持者相信“为了拯救国家可以破坏规则”，从而将对民主制度的暴力行为合法化。

这种模式在任何一个男本位的权力结构中都屡见不鲜：通过定义一个虚构的敌人，让共谋者在恐惧中交出判断力，最后在制度的废墟上建立一个只有“强权”才是真理的秩序。所谓的“民主危机”，本质上是一场关于谁能定义现实的存在性战争。

This piece attempts to analyze Trump's manipulation of elections through 'demonization' and 'conspiracy theories,' but it falls into a typical liberal trap: viewing this as a personal trick of a politician rather than a mature process of Weaponisation of Expression.

Trump's operational path is textbook: first, create cognitive entry points through religion and fear, defining opponents as 'non-human' threats; then, 'flood the media with sewage'—overwhelming the public space with massive, low-quality, and contradictory misinformation. This isn't an argument over facts; it's the destruction of the very concept of 'fact.' When people stop caring about what is true and only care about who is speaking for their camp, the power of interpretation shifts entirely from rational logic to the game of identity politics.

The deeper horror lies in the structural violence. The potential abuse of the 1887 Electoral Count Act proves that the 'sewage' at the cultural layer is merely the appetizer. The ultimate goal is the illegal seizure of power through institutional means. This is a classic operation of Meta Violence: using narratives to convince supporters that 'breaking rules is necessary to save the nation,' thereby legitimizing violent acts against democratic institutions.

This pattern is ubiquitous in any masculine-centric power structure: define a fictional enemy, make co-conspirators surrender their judgment in fear, and finally establish an order where only 'might' is right upon the ruins of the system. The so-called 'democratic crisis' is, in essence, an existential war over who gets to define reality.
