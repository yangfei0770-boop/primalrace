核协议的定价权：一场关于“文明”掩体下的性别化掠夺The Pricing of Nuclear Power: Gendered Plunder Under the Mask of 'Civilization'

国际 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-23 § 链接
所谓核不扩散原则，不过是强权用来定义谁有资格持有暴力的文化武器。
Non-proliferation principles are merely cultural weapons used by hegemons to define who is entitled to violence.

特朗普及他的“交易艺术”，本质上是在进行一场关于暴力解释权的重新定价。在面对伊朗时，核不扩散是不可逾越的“文明”底线；而面对沙特时，同样的底线在数十亿美元的核电订单面前瞬间坍塌。这再次证明，所谓的国际准则、人权或安全叙事，从来不是为了公正，而是为了在结构性暴力中为特定阵营制造合法性掩体。

这场交易揭示了典型的元暴力（meta violence）逻辑：定义谁是“文明的伙伴”，谁是“邪恶的轴心”，其核心不在于行为本身，而在于谁掌握了定义权的定价权。特朗普及沙特王储的共谋，是两种君主制权力欲望的镜像投射——他们追求的不是安全，而是对暴力的绝对垄断。而伊朗被推向绝路，是因为它在这一套男本位、强权中心的叙事中被定义为“客体”，被剥夺了定义自身安全的权利。

最讽刺的是，这场战争的成本——数以百亿计的美元——被用来维持一个毫无意义的暴力循环，而这些资金足以覆盖数百万低收入者的医疗与住房。这种资源分配的极度扭曲，正是结构性暴力（structural violence）的具象化：底层民众的生存权在强权者的“面子工程”和核武器博弈中被视作可忽略的噪音。所谓的“解放”或“安全”，不过是武器化叙事在掩盖一次又一次的掠夺。

Trump and his 'Art of the Deal' are essentially re-pricing the interpretation of violence. When dealing with Iran, non-proliferation is an insurmountable 'civilized' bottom line; yet, facing Saudi Arabia, that same line collapses instantly before multi-billion dollar nuclear contracts. This proves that international norms, human rights, and security narratives are never about justice, but about creating a legitimacy mask for structural violence.

This deal reveals the core logic of meta violence: defining who is a 'civilized partner' and who is an 'axis of evil' depends not on behavior, but on who holds the pricing power over the definition. The complicity between Trump and the Saudi Crown Prince is a mirror projection of two monarchical desires for power—they seek not security, but an absolute monopoly on violence. Iran is pushed to the brink because it is defined as the 'object' in this masculine-centric, power-driven narrative, stripped of the right to define its own security.

Most tragically, the cost of this war—tens of billions of dollars—is spent maintaining a meaningless cycle of violence, while those funds could cover healthcare and housing for millions. This distorted resource allocation is the embodiment of structural violence: the survival rights of the marginalized are treated as negligible noise in the face of hegemonic 'face-saving' and nuclear gambling. The so-called 'liberation' or 'security' is nothing more than a weaponized narrative masking continuous plunder.
