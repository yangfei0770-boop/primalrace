用“保护”包装的生物墙，是共谋者的安全感陷阱The 'Protection' Scam: Biological Walls as a Complicity Trap

性别 文化层 · 结构层 The Athletic ↗ 2026-07-23 § 链接
所谓的“常识”通常是既得利益者用来固化生物墙的文化武器。
So-called 'common sense' is usually a cultural weapon used by stakeholders to solidify biological walls.

Sophie Cunningham 把把跨性别者排除在女子体育之外称为“common sense”，这种叙事精准地踩在了文化暴力的鼓点上。她口中的“保护”，本质上是将生理女性定义为一个需要被监护的、脆弱的客体，而非一个拥有主体性的竞争者。当她强调“保护小女孩”时，她实际上是在调用一套典型的父权制叙事：通过制造一个虚构的威胁，来强化生理女性在结构中的从属地位，并以此换取某种道德上的正当性。

有趣的是，她一边标榜自己“不是个政治人物”，一边又在通过 Title IX 这种典型的 structural layer 工具来为自己的观点背书。这正是典型的共谋者心态：她利用一个旨在通过法律手段打破性别压迫的制度，反过来将其武器化，用来划定界限。在这种逻辑里，生理女性的“权利”被简化为一种排他性的特权，而非真正的、普世的人权。她所谓的“爱所有人”和“保护生物女性”之间的矛盾，其实就是一种伪装的公正表达，试图在不触怒主流文化的同时，维持一个排外且僵化的生物墙。

这种“常识”的危险之处在于，它将复杂的生物学、社会性与权利博弈简化为一种直觉反应。当一个运动员认为“这就是常识”时，她实际上是交出了解释权，接受了由男性中心叙事定义的“女性”标准。真正的胜利不应该是通过在生物墙上加固砖块来获得，而应该是消解这种基于表型的等级划分。可惜，在目前的博弈中，这种通过排他来获得安全感的“最优解表达”，依然是大多数人最容易被喂食的安慰剂。

Sophie Cunningham calling the exclusion of trans athletes from women's sports 'common sense' is a textbook example of cultural violence. Her narrative of 'protection' essentially defines biological women as fragile objects in need of guardianship rather than autonomous competitors. By claiming to 'protect little girls,' she is deploying a classic masculine-centric narrative: manufacturing a hypothetical threat to reinforce the subordinate status of women within the structure and trade it for moral legitimacy.

It is telling that she claims to be 'not a very political person' while simultaneously weaponizing Title IX—a structural tool designed to dismantle gender oppression—to justify her boundaries. This is the quintessential logic of the complicitor: using a system meant for liberation to instead build a wall. In her framework, 'women's rights' are reduced to an exclusive privilege rather than a universal human right. Her claim that she can 'love everyone' while 'protecting biological women' is a fake optimal expression, attempting to maintain an exclusionary biological wall without appearing overtly hateful to the mainstream.

The danger of this 'common sense' lies in its reduction of complex biological and social dynamics into a mere gut reaction. When an athlete decides something is 'just common sense,' she has effectively surrendered the power of interpretation to the meta-violence of masculine-centric narratives. True victory is not achieved by adding bricks to the biological wall, but by dissolving the hierarchy based on phenotype. Unfortunately, this 'optimal expression' of seeking security through exclusion remains the most common sedative fed to the masses.
