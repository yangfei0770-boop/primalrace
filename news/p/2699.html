食谱里的“优雅”与被抹除的无偿劳动The 'Elegance' of Recipes and the Erasure of Unpaid Labor

哲学 文化层 · 结构层 The New York Times ↗ 2026-07-22 § 链接
所谓的“简单优雅”是建立在对具体劳动成本的结构性抹除之上。
So-called 'simple elegance' is built upon the structural erasure of actual labor costs.

一篇 25 分钟的 Shrimp Scampi 食谱，被包装成一种“优雅” (elegant) 的生活方式。但在 NYT 这种中产阶级叙事里，所谓的“简单”是一个典型的 weaponized concept。它通过定义结果的快捷，掩盖了前置的结构性暴力：谁在剥虾 (peeled and deveined)？谁在洗涤那些为了追求“优雅”而产生的琐碎餐具？

在这种文化层面的表达中，烹饪被简化为一种- 审美体验和- 认知入口。评论区里的用户在讨论如何通过减少 1/2 杯水来优化口感，这种博弈是典型的“中产阶级最优解”——他们在讨论如何让结果更完美，却完全不触碰这个结果背后的 labor cost。这种对劳动的视而不见，正是元暴力的一种延伸：将女性在私人领域承担的无偿家务劳动，通过“食谱”这种文化产品，转化为一种可消费的、轻盈的“品味” (Taste)。

当一个食谱被标记为 5 星且“简单”时，它实际上是在共谋一种叙事：即这些准备工作是不计入时间的，或者是可以被轻易外包的。这种抹除让人们忘记了，在“优雅”的餐盘之下，是无数被规训为“照顾者”的女性在进行着无形的、不被定价的结构性劳动。所谓的 elegant，本质上是一种对劳动的傲慢。

A 25-minute Shrimp Scampi recipe is packaged as an 'elegant' lifestyle. In the bourgeois narrative of the NYT, 'simplicity' is a weaponized concept. By defining the shortcut to the result, it masks the structural violence of the prerequisites: Who peeled and deveined the shrimp? Who washes the tedious dishes required to maintain this 'elegance'?

In this cultural expression, cooking is reduced to an aesthetic experience and a cognitive entry point. Commenters gamble on how to optimize taste by reducing 1/2 cup of water—a typical 'middle-class optimal expression.' They argue over making the result more perfect while completely ignoring the labor cost. This invisibility of labor is an extension of meta-violence: transforming the unpaid domestic labor of women in the private sphere into a consumable, lightweight 'Taste' via cultural products like recipes.

When a recipe is tagged as 5-star and 'easy,' it is complicit in a narrative that preparation time is negligible or easily outsourced. This erasure makes us forget that beneath the 'elegant' plate lies the invisible, unpriced structural labor of countless women conditioned as 'caregivers.' What they call 'elegant' is, in essence, an arrogance toward labor.
