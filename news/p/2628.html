关税、囚笼与‘文明’的暴力闭环Tariffs, Cages, and the Closed Loop of 'Civilized' Violence

国际 直接层 · 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-21 § 链接
所有的‘国家利益’叙事，本质上都是元暴力在不同层级的同步投放。
All 'National Interest' narratives are essentially the synchronized deployment of meta-violence across different layers.

这篇新闻是一个典型的暴力三角样本。最表层是直接暴力 (direct violence)：在约旦和伊拉克的士兵死亡，以及被剥夺体重 30 磅、在移民拘留所遭受非人待遇的萨尔苏尔。但请注意，这些血肉之躯的损耗被包裹在‘反恐’、‘国家安全’或‘外交政策’这些文化层 (cultural layer) 的武器化叙事中。萨尔苏尔的经历证明了：无论是巴勒斯坦的监狱还是美国的拘留所，只要权力逻辑是‘去人性化’的，其操作手册完全通用。

结构层 (structural layer) 的暴力则体现在 Trump 激活的 1930 年《Smoot-Hawley》法案残余。这不只是贸易战，而是一种典型的元暴力 (meta violence) 逻辑：通过单方面重新定义‘公平’和‘歧视’，将贸易协议变成可以随时撕毁的废纸。白宫声称要让加拿大‘负责’，这种‘负责’的定义权完全掌握在施暴者手中。当一个人掌握了定义事实的权力，他就可以把掠夺包装成‘正义’，把破坏包装成‘保护 hardworking Americans’。

最令人作呕的共谋 (complicity) 在于，无论是在加拿大的政治光谱中，还是在美国的民主党内部，人们在讨论的是‘关税额度’或‘外交失误’，而很少有人将这些经济震荡与那些在拘留所里被羞辱的个体联系起来。因为在男性中心叙事的元暴力下，‘大棋局’的宏大叙事永远优先于个体的存在性。士兵的死是‘牺牲’，移民的苦是‘程序’，而关税的波动是‘博弈’。在这种共谋中，人的主体性被彻底抹除，变成了一个可以被交易的数字或一个可以被牺牲的筹码。

This news is a textbook sample of the Violence Triangle. At the direct layer, we see the deaths of soldiers in Jordan and Iraq, and the dehumanization of Salah Sarsour, who lost 30lbs in immigration custody. Crucially, this physical attrition is wrapped in weaponized cultural narratives like 'counter-terrorism' and 'national security.' Sarsour's experience proves that whether in a Palestinian prison or a US detention center, the operational manual is identical as long as the power logic is based on dehumanization.

The structural violence manifests in Trump's activation of the 1930 Smoot-Hawley era legislation. This isn't just a trade war; it is the logic of meta-violence: redefining 'fairness' and 'discrimination' unilaterally to turn trade agreements into scrap paper. The White House demands Canada be 'held accountable,' but the power to define that accountability rests solely with the aggressor. When one controls the definition of reality, plunder is rebranded as 'justice' and destruction as 'protecting hardworking Americans.'

The most sickening complicity lies in how politicians across the spectrum—from Canada to the US Democrats—discuss 'tariff rates' or 'diplomatic failures' while ignoring the link between economic shocks and the humiliated individuals in cages. Under the masculine-centric narrative of meta-violence, the 'Grand Strategy' always takes precedence over existential presence. The death of a soldier is 'sacrifice,' the suffering of an immigrant is 'procedure,' and tariff fluctuations are 'game theory.' In this complicity, human subjectivity is erased, reduced to a tradable digit or a disposable pawn.
