关税游戏：用“国家利益”掩盖的结构性掠夺The Tariff Game: Structural Plunder Masked as National Interest

国际 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The New York Times ↗ 2026-07-24 § 链接
关税不是贸易工具，而是统治者通过制造混乱夺取定价权的武器。
Tariffs are not trade tools, but weapons for rulers to seize pricing power by manufacturing chaos.

特朗普在 18 个月内 45 次调整关税，平均每 12 天变一次。这种频率已经脱离了经济逻辑，进入了纯粹的“武器化表达”领域。所谓的贸易战，本质上是一场认知入口的争夺战：通过频繁地定义谁是“敌人”、谁需要被“惩罚”，将复杂的全球供应链简化为一种幼稚的、基于身份政治的胜负游戏。

从加尔通的暴力三角来看，这是典型的 structural violence。关税在账面上由进口商支付，但最终通过价格传导，由美国普通家庭每年承担 1100 美元的额外成本。这种暴力是弥散且隐形的，它不产生血腥场面，但它通过削减底层民众的 Actual 资源（购买力），扩大了 Potential 与 Actual 之间的差额。而文化层面的暴力则在同步运作——用“美国优先”的宏大叙事，掩盖这种对内收税、对外破坏的 scam。

最荒诞的是，这种操作在法律上已涉嫌违宪。当最高法院的裁决被无视，所谓的“常识”被抛弃，我们看到的其实是元暴力 (meta violence) 的升级：统治者不再满足于制定不公平的规则，而是直接宣布规则本身不再适用。在这种叙事下，经济运行的逻辑被权力意志所取代，而普通消费者在这一博弈中，仅仅是被定价权剥夺的客体。

Trump has adjusted tariffs 45 times in 18 months—averaging once every 12 days. This frequency has transcended economic logic and entered the realm of weaponized expression. The so-called trade war is essentially a battle for cognitive entry: by constantly defining who is the 'enemy' and who deserves 'punishment,' the administration simplifies complex global supply chains into a naive, identity-politics-driven game of win or lose.

Through the lens of the Violence Triangle, this is textbook structural violence. On paper, tariffs are paid by importers, but the cost is passed down, forcing the average American family to swallow an annual $1,100 burden. This violence is diffuse and invisible; it produces no blood, but it erodes the Actual resources of the working class, widening the gap between Potential and Actual. Simultaneously, cultural violence operates in the background—using the grand narrative of 'America First' to mask a scam that taxes its own people while dismantling global partnerships.

Most absurdly, these actions defy the Supreme Court. When judicial rulings are ignored and 'common sense' is discarded, we are witnessing an escalation of meta-violence: the ruler is no longer content with crafting unfair rules, but declares that the rules themselves are irrelevant. In this narrative, economic logic is replaced by the will to power, leaving the average consumer as a mere object stripped of all pricing power.
