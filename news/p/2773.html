电影业的“革命”不过是认知入口的权力交接The 'Revolution' of Cinema is Merely a Handover of Cognitive Gateways

哲学 结构层 · 文化层 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-23 § 链接
所谓的行业创新，本质上是资本在发现旧叙事失效后，对新流量入口的投机性共谋。
The so-called innovation is just opportunistic complicity by capital after realizing old narratives have failed.

《卫报》把 2020 年代的电影业描述成一场“革命”，这真是典型的文化暴力式叙事——用“创新”这个词来掩盖资本在面对流量崩塌时的绝望投机。所谓的革命，无非是好莱坞这群迟钝的权力持有者终于意识到，他们过去垄断的认知入口（Theatrical Experience）失效了，于是赶紧地向 YouTube 和 TikTok 的原住民们低头，试图通过共谋来接管新的解释权。

文中提到的 Curry Barker 或 Kane Parsons 的成功，被包装成“信任原住民”，实际上是资本在进行一次典型的最优解博弈：既然传统的电影学院体系（旧的结构性暴力）已经无法制造能让 Z 世代买单的快感，那么直接购买已经验证过流量的 YouTube 样本就是成本最低的方案。这不是对创作者主体性的认可，而是对“流量数据”的跪拜。当 MrBeast 这种顶级流量操盘手未来接手导演椅时，电影将彻底沦为短视频逻辑的延伸，成为一种被算法精准计算的、缺乏主体性的产品。

最讽刺的是对《索尼克》的吹捧。文章将其视为“倾听观众”的里程碑，但在我看来，这只是一个典型的 weaponized feedback 案例。制片方并没有在乎艺术质量，他们只是在进行一次实时 A/B 测试。当他们发现“丑陋的索尼克”会引发负面舆论从而损害潜在收益时，迅速将其修正。这种所谓的“民主化”生产，本质上是资本在用更高效的算法替代旧的傲慢，从而更精准地收割观众的注意力。

至于那些版权到期后的低成本恐怖片，不过是结构性暴力的余波：在版权法的保护伞下，资本垄断了文化符号的解释权；而当保护期结束，这些符号被粗暴地解构为 slasher movie，这恰恰证明了原先的“神圣性”叙事本身就是一场巨大的 scam。

The Guardian describes the 2020s cinema as a 'revolution'—a classic piece of cultural violence, using the word 'innovation' to mask the desperate speculation of capital facing a collapse of traffic. This revolution is nothing more than the sluggish power-holders of Hollywood realizing their monopoly on the cognitive gateway (Theatrical Experience) has failed, leading them to bow to YouTube and TikTok natives in a bid for a new complicity of interpretive power.

The success of Curry Barker or Kane Parsons is framed as 'trusting natives,' but it is actually a classic game of optimal expression: since the traditional film school system (an old structural violence) can no longer produce thrills that Z-generation will pay for, purchasing pre-validated YouTube samples is the lowest-cost solution. This is not an endorsement of the creator's subjectivity, but a prostration before 'traffic data.' When a top-tier traffic operator like MrBeast eventually takes the director's chair, cinema will completely become an extension of short-video logic—a product of algorithmic calculation devoid of subjectivity.

The praise for Sonic the Hedgehog is the most ironic part. The article calls it a milestone of 'listening to the audience,' but I see it as a case of weaponized feedback. The producers didn't care about artistic quality; they were performing a real-time A/B test. When they realized the 'ugly Sonic' triggered negative discourse that threatened potential profits, they corrected it. This so-called 'democratization' is simply capital replacing old arrogance with a more efficient algorithm to harvest attention more precisely.

As for those low-budget horror films following copyright expiry, they are merely the aftershocks of structural violence: under the umbrella of copyright law, capital monopolized the interpretive power of cultural symbols. When that protection ends and these symbols are crudely decomposed into slasher movies, it only proves that the original 'sacred' narrative was a massive scam all along.
