关税战、山火与被当作掩体的“喜剧”Tariffs, Wildfires, and Comedy as a Shield

国际 结构层 · 文化层 The New York Times ↗ 2026-07-22 § 链接
用幽默消解权力滥用，本质上是在用文化层面的止痛药掩盖结构性暴力的创口。
Using humor to dissolve power abuse is merely applying a cultural analgesic to a structural violence wound.

特朗普对加拿大挥舞关税大棒，这在表面上是国家间的贸易博弈，但在底层逻辑中，这是一次典型的通过制造“敌我”叙事来夺取认知入口的权力演习。他利用Smoot-Hawley Act这种陈旧的制度工具，将结构性暴力合法化，把经济压榨包装成所谓的“国家利益”。

而《The Daily Show》等深夜秀的反应，恰恰揭示了文化层面的共谋机制。当Desi Lydic用讽刺的口吻调侃关税和山火时，这种“喜剧化”的处理方式在潜意识里将一场严重的权力滥用降格为一场“闹剧”。喜剧的逻辑是：只要我们能嘲笑它，我们就觉得我们掌控了它。但事实上，这种娱乐化的消解并没有削弱结构层面的暴力，反而为大众提供了一种低成本的心理代偿——我们在笑声中完成了对现实的妥协。

最讽刺的是，这种叙事武器在面对气候变化时表现得尤为无力。特朗普在私人飞机旁“研究”山火烟雾，这种极端的矛盾感正是元暴力的写照：定义权掌握在手中，事实在权力面前可以被随意裁剪。当一个体制可以把污染定义为“需要付费的进口商品”时，逻辑已经死在了权力欲望的祭坛上。

我们不需要在深夜秀里寻找安慰。真正的存在性战争不在于谁的Punchline更响，而在于那个被法律化、制度化的暴力机器，是如何在一次次被嘲笑的“闹剧”中，稳固地完成了对全球资源和生存权的重新定价。

Trump's tariff war on Canada appears as a trade dispute, but fundamentally, it is a power exercise in capturing the cognitive entry point by manufacturing an 'enemy' narrative. By weaponizing the Smoot-Hawley Act, he legitimizes structural violence, packaging economic extortion as 'national interest.'

The reaction from 'The Daily Show' and other late-night hosts reveals a mechanism of cultural complicity. When Desi Lydic satirizes tariffs and wildfires, this 'comedic' framing reduces a severe abuse of power to a mere 'farce.' The logic of comedy suggests that if we can laugh at it, we have mastered it. In reality, this entertainment-based dissolution does not weaken structural violence; it provides a low-cost psychological compensation—we concede to reality through laughter.

This narrative weapon is most impotent when facing climate change. Trump 'investigating' wildfire smoke while standing next to his idling jet is a textbook manifestation of meta-violence: the power to define reality and crop facts to suit desire. When a regime can define pollution as an 'import product requiring payment,' logic is sacrificed on the altar of power.

We don't need solace from late-night shows. The real existential war isn't about whose punchline is louder, but about how a legalized, institutionalized violence machine solidifies its grip on global resources and survival rights through the very 'farces' we are told to laugh at.
