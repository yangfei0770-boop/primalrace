用“创意写作”掩盖的纯粹暴力Pure Violence Masked as 'Creative Writing'

哲学 直接层 · 文化层 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-21 § 链接
将残杀客体化为“不幸的巧合”，是元暴力最典型的叙事伪装。
Turning the slaughter of others into 'unfortunate coincidences' is the classic narrative camouflage of meta-violence.

这起案件的荒诞感不在于死者的数量，而在于被告 James Desborough 试图通过一套极其拙劣的叙事，将直接暴力 (direct violence) 转化为一种“意外的发现”。在法庭上，他把肢解尸体描述为一种在药物影响下的“糟糕决定”，把两个朋友的死亡定义为“一系列不幸的巧合”。这不仅是法律意义上的谎言，更是一次典型的存在性博弈：他试图通过扮演一个“混乱且无能的受害者”，来对冲掉他作为施暴者的主体性。

最令人作呕的细节在于他对待尸体的方式——肢解、焚烧、抛弃。这种对肉体的绝对支配，本质上是将他人彻底客体化。当控方提到他喜欢剧集《Dexter》中的肢解场景时，他将其轻描淡写为“看到了广告”。而那句“我是个杀手，准备好执行任何掠食者”的笔记，在被戳穿后被定义为“creative writing”。这种对暴力的审美化和文学化，正是文化暴力 (cultural violence) 的运作方式：通过重新定义事实，试图让残忍的行为在逻辑上变得“合理”或“无关痛痒”。

值得注意的是，他在狱中杀掉室友的行为被他称为“out of character”（不符合性格）。这种表述极其傲慢——在元暴力的逻辑中，他预设了一个“正常的自我”作为基准，而将杀戮视为某种偶然的偏差。事实上，从肢解朋友到杀掉室友，这并非偏差，而是一以贯之的权力实践。他通过剥夺他人的生命和存在，在自己的森林小屋和囚室里建立了一个绝对的统治域。

这场审判目前还在继续，但 Desborough 的辩护词已经揭示了这样一个事实：对于一个习惯于将他人视为可消耗资源的掠食者来说，真相并不重要，重要的是他能否通过操纵叙事，在存在性战争中为自己赢得一张“精神不稳定”的免死金牌。

The absurdity of this case lies not in the death toll, but in James Desborough's clumsy attempt to rebrand direct violence as a series of 'accidental discoveries.' In court, he framed the dismemberment of bodies as 'bad decisions' made under drug influence and defined the deaths of two friends as a 'series of unfortunate circumstances.' This is more than a legal lie; it is a calculated move in an existential war: he attempts to shed his agency as a perpetrator by performing the role of a 'chaotic, powerless victim.'

The most repulsive detail is his treatment of the bodies—dismembering, burning, and discarding. This absolute dominion over flesh is the essence of total objectification. When confronted with his admiration for the dismemberment scenes in *Dexter*, he dismissed it as merely seeing an advertisement. Even more telling is the note where he wrote, 'I’m a killer and ready to execute any predators,' which he later rebranded as 'creative writing.' This aestheticization of violence is precisely how cultural violence operates: by redefining facts to make brutality seem 'logical' or 'insignificant.'

Notably, he described the killing of his cellmate as being 'out of character.' This phrasing is profoundly arrogant—within the logic of meta-violence, he presumes a 'normal self' as the baseline, treating murder as a mere deviation. In reality, from carving up friends to killing a cellmate, there is no deviation; there is only a consistent practice of power. He established an absolute domain of rule in his woodland cabin and his prison cell by stripping others of their existence.

The trial continues, but Desborough’s defense has already exposed a grim truth: for a predator who views others as consumable resources, the truth is irrelevant. What matters is whether he can manipulate the narrative to win a 'get-out-of-jail-free' card based on mental instability.
