家族独裁的最终形态：把“墙”写进法律The Final Form of Family Dictatorship: Codifying the 'Wall'

国际 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-20 § 链接
当操纵选举的成本高于直接取消，独裁者会选择将结构暴力法律化。
When the cost of rigging elections exceeds the benefit, dictators codify structural violence into absolute law.

奥尔特加这次的表态不再需要掩饰，他直接把“不再举行选举”摆在台面上。这在本质上是一次从 structural violence 到 direct violence 的加速演进。过去他还需要通过 rigged election（操纵选举）来维持一个“民主”的文化外壳，但现在他发现，与其在表演中冒险，不如直接通过立法把 opposition（反对派）彻底物理隔绝。所谓的“建立一道墙”，就是典型的表达武器化——他试图通过重新定义“法律”，将所有异见者直接划定为“叛国者”和“政变者”，从而在认知入口上完成合法化屠杀的铺垫。

最讽刺的是，这场家族独裁的权力共谋中，他的妻子穆里略被提升为“共同总统”。这种所谓的“权力共享”并非女权主义的胜利，而是一次极其恶劣的 masculine-centric narrative 伪装。穆里略不是在打破生物墙，而是在成为元暴力的共谋者。她通过扮演一个权力顶端的女性，掩盖了这个政权对底层女性、原住民（如被折磨致死的 Brooklyn Rivera）以及所有非权力阶层实施的系统性掠夺。在这种家族共谋中，女性的权力被异化为一种“特权入场券”，而代价是整个族群的主体性死亡。

这不再是某种政治博弈，而是一场纯粹的存在性战争。当一个政权开始通过剥夺公民权、关闭大学和媒体来制造“真实”时，它其实是在承认自己的 domestic support（国内支持）已经低到必须通过绝对的暴力才能维持。这种“墙”不仅是法律的，更是认知的。他们试图制造一个没有异见可能性的真空地带，让人们在恐惧中内化这种顺从，直到人们相信“没有选举才是安全”。

Ortega has stopped pretending. By announcing the end of elections, he is accelerating the transition from structural violence to direct violence. Previously, he relied on rigged elections to maintain a cultural veneer of 'democracy.' Now, he finds it more efficient to use the law to physically isolate the opposition. His rhetoric of 'building a wall' is a classic example of the weaponisation of expression—redefining 'law' to categorize all dissenters as 'traitors,' thereby preparing the cognitive ground for legalized slaughter.

The most cynical part of this power complicity is the elevation of his wife, Murillo, to 'co-president.' This is not a feminist victory, but a grotesque masquerade of masculine-centric narrative. Murillo is not breaking the biological wall; she is becoming a complicit agent of meta-violence. By performing the role of a powerful woman at the top, she masks the regime's systemic plunder of grassroots women, Indigenous people (such as the tortured Brooklyn Rivera), and all non-privileged classes. In this family complicity, female power is reduced to a 'privilege ticket,' and the price is the death of the collective subjectivity of women.

This is no longer a political game; it is a pure existential war. When a regime strips citizenship and shutters universities to manufacture its own 'truth,' it admits that its domestic support has collapsed to the point where only absolute violence can sustain it. This 'wall' is not just legal, but cognitive. They are attempting to create a vacuum where dissent is unthinkable, forcing the population to internalize submission until they believe that 'the absence of elections equals security.'
