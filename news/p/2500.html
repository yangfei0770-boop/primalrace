蟑螂的尊严与权力的静默The Dignity of Cockroaches and the Silence of Power

国际 元暴力 · 结构层 · 文化层 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-17 § 链接
当权力将民众定义为蟑螂，绝食就是用肉体的消亡来夺回定义权。
When power defines the people as cockroaches, hunger striking is the act of reclaiming identity through physical decay.

最高法院首席大法官把失业青年称为“cockroaches”，这不只是一个冒犯性的词汇，而是一次典型的 meta violence。通过将一个群体定义为“害虫”，权力在文化层完成了对该群体主体性的剥夺：蟑螂不需要人权，蟑螂的生存状态不应被视为“生活”，因此对他们的剥削和忽视在逻辑上变得正当化。这就是元暴力的运作方式——先定义，再通过定义来合法化随后的 structural violence。

CJP 聪明地将这个标签武器化（weaponized），通过自我认同为“蟑螂”来消解对方的攻击性，并将这种被排斥的身份转化为结盟的利器。而 Sonam Wangchuk 的绝食，则是这场存在性战争中最极端的表达。在 Potential（一个公正的教育体系）与 Actual（被单次考试决定终身的残酷现实）的巨大差额中，当所有语言表达都被权力静默时，肉体的衰竭成了唯一的认知入口。

Modi 政府的静默并非无能，而是一种精准的共谋。通过拒绝对话，政府在维持一种“蟑螂不配进入谈判桌”的叙事。他们派遣医生监测 Wangchuk 的体征，不是出于人道，而是在计算一个临界点——在确保他不会在眼皮底下死掉（从而制造出巨大的 direct violence 政治风险）的同时，最大限度地消耗抗争者的意志。这种“温水煮青蛙”的结构性暴力，比直接的逮捕更令人绝望。

绝食者拒绝多用一台风扇，因为“其他人也在忍受酷热”。这种极端的共情是这场博弈中唯一的 Just Expression。当权力试图通过定义对方为“非人”来统治时，抗争者通过共享痛苦重新确立了人的身份。但这场马拉松的残酷在于，肉体是有限的，而权力的静默是无限的。

The Chief Justice calling unemployed youth “cockroaches” is more than a slur; it is a textbook example of meta violence. By defining a group as pests, power strips them of their subjectivity at the cultural layer: cockroaches don't need human rights, and their existence is not a “life.” Thus, their exploitation becomes logically justified. This is how meta violence works—define first, then legitimize structural violence.

The CJP cleverly weaponized this tag, embracing the identity of the “cockroach” to neutralize the attack and turn exclusion into a tool for alliance. Sonam Wangchuk’s hunger strike is the most extreme expression in this existential war. In the gap between the Potential (a just education system) and the Actual (a brutal reality determined by a single three-hour test), when all linguistic expression is silenced by power, the decay of the body becomes the only remaining cognitive entry point.

The Modi government’s silence is not incompetence, but a precise form of complicity. By refusing to engage, the government maintains the narrative that “cockroaches do not belong at the negotiating table.” Sending doctors to monitor Wangchuk is not humanitarianism; it is a calculation of the threshold—ensuring he doesn't die (which would create a direct violence political risk) while maximizing the exhaustion of the protesters' will. This structural violence is more despairing than a direct arrest.

The hunger striker’s refusal of an extra fan because “everyone else is suffering” is the only Just Expression in this game. While power attempts to rule by defining the other as “non-human,” the protesters reclaim their humanity by sharing pain. Yet, the cruelty of this marathon is that the body is finite, while the silence of power is infinite.
