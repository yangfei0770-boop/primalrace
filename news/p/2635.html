用“支配”掩盖的生态共谋：狼与人的权力剧本Ecological Complicity Masked by 'Dominance': The Power Script of Wolves and Humans

哲学 文化层 · 结构层 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-21 § 链接
将生存博弈定义为“支配”，是典型的男性中心叙事对自然的殖民。
Defining survival games as 'dominance' is a classic colonial application of masculine-centric narratives to nature.

丹麦政府给游客的指南里，最刺眼的是一个词：show dominance（展示支配）。面对狼，官方建议是盯着它看、大声叫、挥动手臂，直到在对方眼中确立“支配地位”。这种叙事极其典型——它把一次生物性的生存博弈，直接套用了男性中心叙事（masculine-centric narrative）的权力逻辑。在元暴力的潜意识里，世界被简化为“支配者”与“被支配者”，任何冲突的解决路径都被预设为权力的压制，而非物种间的边界共识。

有趣的是，这套指南在保护狼（EU law）的同时，却在教人们如何通过“最大化抵抗”来维持人类的统治地位。这是一种典型的结构性共谋：政府在文化层面上维持“动物保护者”的进步形象，但在行为指令上却在强化“强者统治”的丛林法则。这种矛盾揭示了一个事实：即便是在最文明的北欧，人们对“安全”的定义依然建立在对他者的压制之上。

而那些建议孩子由成人陪同的条款，本质上是在重复一种生物墙的保护逻辑——将弱小者（儿童）隔离在支配博弈之外。但真正的问题在于，当年轻的狼因为接触游客而“降低恐惧距离”时，这其实是一场关于认知入口的误判。人类用“喂食”或“好奇”邀请狼进入社交场，却在狼真正响应邀请时，立刻掏出“支配”的剧本要求对方臣服。这不叫指导，这叫用殖民者的逻辑在制造冲突。

The most glaring part of the Danish government's guide is the phrase: 'show dominance.' Facing a wolf, the official advice is to stare, shout, and wave arms to establish a 'dominant position.' This is a textbook example of applying a masculine-centric narrative to a biological survival game. In the subconscious of meta-violence, the world is simplified into 'dominators' and 'the dominated,' where the only path to resolving conflict is the suppression of power rather than a consensus on inter-species boundaries.

Interestingly, while protecting wolves via EU law, the guide instructs humans to maintain their ruling status through 'maximum resistance.' This is a structural complicity: the government maintains a progressive image as 'animal protectors' at the cultural layer, while reinforcing the jungle law of 'stronger rules' in its behavioral directives. It reveals that even in the most 'civilized' Nordic societies, the definition of 'safety' is still built upon the suppression of the Other.

Furthermore, the requirement for children to be accompanied by adults merely replicates a protective logic based on the biological wall—isolating the weak from the game of dominance. The real issue is the misjudgment of cognitive entry points. Humans invite wolves into a social space through feeding or curiosity, but the moment the wolf responds to that invitation, humans suddenly produce a script of 'dominance' and demand submission. This isn't guidance; it's the art of manufacturing conflict using a colonizer's logic.
