以“增长”之名，为战争机器续命的叙事骗局The Growth Scam: Reviving the War Machine through Economic Narrative

国际 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-22 § 链接
所谓的“国防驱动增长”是典型的武器化叙事，用经济术语掩盖结构性暴力。
Labeling defense spending as 'economic growth' is a weaponized narrative masking systemic violence.

Wes Streeting 和 Andy Burnham 正在向英国公众兜售一个巨大的 scam：把增加军费包装成“经济增长”和“再工业化”的引擎。这套逻辑极其阴险，它试图将军工复合体（Military-Industrial Complex）的利润，通过“创造就业”和“岗位增长”这种认知入口，转化为普通民众可以接受的福利叙事。

从加尔通的暴力三角来看，这不仅是 structural violence，更是典型的 cultural violence。当政府宣布军费优先级高于医疗和教育时，实际上是在进行一次残酷的资源重新分配。Streeting 提到的“机会成本” (opportunity cost) 才是真相：为了让 BAE Systems 这种军火巨头获得“信心”和“清晰度”，被牺牲掉的是公共卫生系统和基础教育。这种资源挪用直接导致了 Potential − Actual 的差额扩大——本可以提升国民健康和教育水平的资源，被转化成了能杀人的无人机和导弹。

最讽刺的是，这种“增长”是建立在元暴力 (meta violence) 之上的。战争叙事永远是男性中心叙事的最高形式：它将人类身体降格为可消耗的工具，将破坏定义为“能力”，将杀戮定义为“国防”。在这种叙事中，女性和弱势群体再次被客体化，成为这种“增长”背后被忽视的代价。至于 Streeting 对以色列战争罪行的轻微批评，不过是某种表演性的政治姿态，旨在维持一个“文明”的伪装，好让这台杀戮机器在合法性的外壳下继续运转。

所谓的“再工业化”如果只能通过制造武器来实现，那么这种工业化本质上是对人类生存空间的殖民。这不是增长，而是对暴力的投资。

Wes Streeting and Andy Burnham are selling the UK public a massive scam: framing increased defense spending as an engine for 'economic growth' and 'reindustrialization.' This logic is insidious, attempting to channel the profits of the Military-Industrial Complex through the cognitive entry points of 'job creation' and 'growth' to make it palatable to the masses.

Applying the Violence Triangle, this is not just structural violence, but blatant cultural violence. When a government prioritizes the military over health and education, it is executing a brutal redistribution of resources. The 'opportunity cost' mentioned by Streeting is the real story: the confidence and clarity provided to arms giants like BAE Systems are bought with the decay of public health and education. This reallocation widens the gap between Potential and Actual—resources that could have elevated national well-being are instead converted into drones and missiles.

Most ironically, this 'growth' is rooted in meta violence. War narratives are the ultimate expression of the masculine-centric narrative: they degrade human bodies into consumable tools, define destruction as 'capability,' and frame killing as 'defense.' In this framework, women and marginalized groups are once again objectified as the invisible costs of this 'growth.' Streeting's mild criticism of Israeli war crimes is mere performative politics, a veneer of 'civilization' designed to keep the killing machine running under a cloak of legitimacy.

If 'reindustrialization' can only be achieved by manufacturing weapons, then such industry is essentially a colonization of human survival space. This is not growth; it is an investment in violence.
