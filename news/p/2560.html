好莱坞的“奥德赛”：一场关于风景的殖民骗局Hollywood's Odyssey: A Colonial Scam of Landscape

国际 结构层 · 文化层 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-20 § 链接
电影带来的不是关注，而是对真实空间的解释权掠夺与资源榨取。
Cinema brings not attention, but the seizure of interpretative power and resource extraction.

Christopher Nolan 拍的是《奥德赛》，但给 Messenia 留下的将是一场典型的 weaponized 叙事。好莱坞的逻辑很简单：将一个真实且脆弱的地理空间抽干，将其简化为一张“电影名片”或一个“认知入口”。当观众在银幕上看到那些壮丽的海岸线时，他们消费的不是历史，而是一种被精心包装的、去主体化的“景观”。

这背后是极具欺骗性的共谋。Temes 这样的奢侈度假村开发商将自己包装成“eco-friendly”，但实际上，在水资源匮乏的地区建立四个高尔夫球场和无数泳池，这就是最典型的 structural violence。他们通过定义什么是“高端旅游”，在物理上剥夺当地社区的生存资源，在文化上通过“旅游单一文化”抹除地区的独特性。这种“发展”本质上是一种殖民：外来资本定义价值 $\rightarrow$ 资源向定义者倾斜 $\rightarrow$ 原生社区在被榨干后被抛弃。

真正有价值的表达，是那些试图重启废弃铁路、复兴传统陶艺的志愿者和集体。他们追求的是 human-scale 的存在，而非被好莱坞聚光灯瞬间点燃又迅速烧尽的快餐式关注。当一个地方被定义为“电影取景地”时，它的 Potential（真实的历史与生态）与 Actual（被旅游业异化的现状）之间的差额就被极大地拉开了。这种差额，就是由资本与权力共同制造的暴力。

Christopher Nolan is filming The Odyssey, but what he leaves for Messenia is a classic case of weaponized narrative. The Hollywood logic is simple: drain a real, fragile geographical space and flatten it into a "cinematic postcard" or a cognitive entry point. When audiences see those magnificent coastlines, they aren't consuming history, but a meticulously packaged, dehumanized "spectacle."

Behind this lies a deceptive complicity. Developers like Temes mask themselves as "eco-friendly," yet building four golf courses and countless pools in a water-scarce region is a textbook example of structural violence. By defining what constitutes "luxury tourism," they physically deprive local communities of survival resources and culturally erase regional uniqueness through a "tourism monoculture." This "development" is essentially colonization: foreign capital defines value $\rightarrow$ resources shift toward the definer $\rightarrow$ the native community is discarded after being bled dry.

The only just expressions are found in the volunteers and collectives attempting to reopen abandoned railways and revive traditional pottery. They seek a human-scale existence, not the flash-in-the-pan attention of a Hollywood spotlight. When a place is defined as a "filming location," the gap between its Potential (authentic history and ecology) and its Actual (the alienated reality of tourism) widens. This gap is the violence manufactured by the complicity of capital and power.
