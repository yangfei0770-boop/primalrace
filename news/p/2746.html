主权是给平民的安慰剂，而共谋者在分赃Sovereignty is a Placebo; The Complicit are Splitting the Loot

国际 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-23 § 链接
所谓的“国家困境”不过是权力的共谋场，平民是唯一的牺牲品。
The so-called 'national predicament' is merely a complicity field where civilians are the only casualties.

这篇文章把伊拉克的现状描述成在美伊之间“进退两难”的 predicament，这简直是典型的 masculine-centric narrative。把一个国家的命运写成两个强权博弈的棋局，这种叙事本身就是一种 cultural violence，它掩盖了最核心的真相：伊拉克的“弱势”不是被动地被夹击，而是一场极其精准的内部共谋。

看看那些所谓的“安全官员”和“政府顾问”：他们一边在高级餐厅里感叹主权被侵犯，一边在通过买卖军职、贪污油款来完成自己的资本积累。当安全局长花 500 万美元买一个职位时，他的最优解表达不再是“保护国家”，而是“保护投资”。这种 structural violence 将国家机器异化成了个体的提款机，而这种共谋在两边都存在——无论是投靠华盛顿的新总理，还是誓死效忠德黑兰的民兵，他们争夺的从来不是什么“主权”或“宗教义务”，而是对资源分配的定价权。

最令人作呕的 scam 是那些“意识形态驱动”的民兵。他们一边宣称为了宗教使命而攻击本国的石油设施，一边在现实中通过建筑和农业投资建立自己的经济帝国。这根本不是什么存在性战争，而是一次拙劣的武器化表达：用“圣战”的叙事给抢劫和破坏贴金，让巴斯拉那些呼吸着毒气、喝着苦水的平民在绝望中产生一种“我们在反抗剥削”的错觉。

在这种 meta-violence 的笼罩下，伊拉克的平民被剥夺了主体性，成为了一个被消费的背景板。他们被告知这是一个“不可能的困境”，但实际上，这个困境是统治阶级通过共谋精心维持的。因为只要战争和混乱持续，只要美伊继续在伊拉克境内博弈，那些中间商、军火商和贪腐官员就能在“平衡”中获得最大化收益。所谓的“主权”，不过是他们用来安抚大众、在外交场合表演的 PR 词汇而已。

This article describes Iraq's situation as an 'impossible predicament' trapped between the US and Iran. This is a classic masculine-centric narrative. Framing a nation's fate as a chessboard for two superpowers is a form of cultural violence; it masks the core truth: Iraq's 'weakness' is not a passive entrapment, but a precisely engineered internal complicity.

Look at the so-called 'security officials' and 'government advisers.' They lament the violation of sovereignty in upscale restaurants while simultaneously accumulating capital through the sale of military ranks and the embezzlement of oil revenues. When a security chief pays $5 million for a command, his optimal expression is no longer 'protecting the state,' but 'protecting the investment.' This structural violence transforms the state apparatus into a personal ATM, and this complicity exists on both sides—whether it is the new Prime Minister courting Washington or the militias swearing loyalty to Tehran. They are not fighting for 'sovereignty' or 'religious duty,' but for the pricing power over resource distribution.

The most disgusting scam is the 'ideologically driven' militias. They claim to attack their own oil infrastructure for a religious mission while building their own economic empires through construction and agriculture. This is not an existential war; it is a clumsy weaponization of expression: using the narrative of 'Holy War' to glaze over robbery and destruction, tricking the civilians of Basra—who breathe toxins and drink brackish water—into a delusion that they are 'fighting exploitation.'

Under this meta-violence, the Iraqi people are stripped of their subjectivity, becoming mere background props. They are told this is an 'impossible predicament,' but in reality, this predicament is meticulously maintained by the ruling class. As long as the war continues and the US and Iran keep gambling in Iraq, the middlemen, arms dealers, and corrupt officials maximize their gains in the 'equilibrium.' 'Sovereignty' is nothing more than a PR term used to appease the masses and perform in diplomatic circles.
