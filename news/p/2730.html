所谓的“苏联文化”不过是元暴力的制服版The So-called 'Soviet Culture' is Just Meta-Violence in Uniform

国际 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-22 § 链接
所有对“管理文化”的批判，本质上都是在讨论谁拥有定义牺牲的权力。
Any critique of 'management culture' is essentially a debate over who owns the power to price sacrifice.

人们在讨论 Mykhailo Drapatyi 的上位时，习惯性地将其描述为“人道”与“苏联式指挥文化”的对立。但请记住，在军队这种极端的暴力机器中，所谓的“文化”从来不是什么礼仪或氛围，而是对生命定价权的分配。Syrskyi 式的“苏联文化”——那种依赖高昂代价的正面强攻、将士兵生命视为可消耗资源的微操，本质上就是一种典型的 masculine-centric narrative。它将身体降格为达成宏大目标的工具，而这种工具化正是所有元暴力的核心逻辑。

Drapatyi 被寄予厚望，是因为他尝试将权力下放给基层，承认反馈，甚至在惨剧发生时选择承担责任而非向下甩锅。这在 structural 层面上是对暴力三角差额的某种修正：他试图缩小“被命令者”与“决策者”之间那种由于信息不对称而产生的暴力差额。但不要被“人道”这个词给 scam 了。在战争状态下，任何指挥官的“最优解表达”都必须在“赢得战争”和“保存生命”之间博弈。当他被赋予总司令的权力，他将面对的是一个巨大的共谋场域——国防部与总参谋部的长期内耗，以及政治性动员的僵局。

这次人事变动是否是 good_news？如果它仅仅是用一个“好人”的形象替换掉一个“暴君”的形象，那这只是 cultural 层的 PR 换皮。真正的胜利在于，基层士兵是否真的拿回了关于自身生存的解释权，还是说，他们只是在期待一个更温柔的屠夫。毕竟，只要军队依然运行在“用人命交换地理位置”的底层逻辑上，所谓的“去苏联化”就只是在元暴力的框架内更换了一套更现代的制服。

The discourse surrounding Mykhailo Drapatyi’s appointment frames it as a clash between 'humanity' and 'Soviet-style command culture.' But let's be clear: in a violent machine like the military, 'culture' is never about atmosphere—it is about the distribution of the power to price human life. The 'Soviet culture' associated with Syrskyi—costly frontal assaults and the treatment of soldiers as disposable assets—is a textbook masculine-centric narrative. It reduces the body to a tool for a grand narrative, which is the very core of meta-violence.

Drapatyi is welcomed because he pushes authority down to junior officers and accepts accountability instead of shifting blame. Structurally, this is an attempt to shrink the violence gap created by information asymmetry between the commanded and the commander. However, do not be scammed by the word 'humane.' In a state of war, any commander's optimal expression is a gamble between 'winning' and 'preserving life.' As he enters the top job, he steps into a massive field of complicity—the unresolved friction between the defense ministry and the general staff, and the political deadlock of mobilization.

Is this good_news? If it is merely replacing a 'tyrant' with a 'nice guy,' it is nothing more than a cultural PR skin-swap. A true victory would be the subordinates reclaiming the power of interpretation over their own existence. Otherwise, we are simply upgrading the aesthetic of the machine. As long as the underlying logic remains 'trading lives for geography,' this 'de-Sovietization' is just meta-violence wearing a more modern uniform.
