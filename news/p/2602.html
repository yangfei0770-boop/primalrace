被丢弃的洋葱与被掩盖的劳动The Discarded Onion and the Erased Labor

哲学 文化层 · 结构层 The New York Times ↗ 2026-07-20 § 链接
任何被定义为“简单”的优雅，都是在抹除具体的劳动过程。
Any elegance defined as "simplicity" is merely the erasure of concrete labor.

一条番茄酱食谱，一个关于“极简”的叙事。Marcella Hazan 的名声在于她告诉世界：不需要复杂的香料，只需要黄油、洋葱和番茄。这种叙事在文化层（cultural layer）被包装成一种“纯粹”的审美，让人们在追求“食材原味”的同时，产生了一种掌握了生活真谛的错觉。但请注意，这种所谓的“简单”其实是一次精巧的武器化表达——它将烹饪从一种复杂的生物化学博弈，简化为了一个可以通过指令完成的 a-to-b 过程。

最讽刺的细节在于 Step 3：Discard the onion（丢弃洋葱）。洋葱在整个炖煮过程中提供了底层的风味支撑，承担了最脏最累的化学反应，但最后它被要求在呈现结果前消失。这简直是结构性暴力（structural violence）在厨房里的微缩版：某些元素被定义为“必要的支撑”，但它们不配进入最终的“成果展示”空间。洋葱的消失，是为了让主菜看起来更“轻盈”，更符合某种中产阶级的审美预期。

评论区里那些试图通过增加 pancetta 或鸡胸肉来“优化”的人，本质上是在进行一场关于“最优解表达”的博弈。他们试图在既定的规则（recipe）中寻找自己的主体性，但依然在共谋一个逻辑：即认为这种由他人定义的“标准答案”才是基准。真正的公正表达不应该是对某个权威食谱的依附，而是承认：无论是黄油的浓郁还是洋葱的牺牲，都是具体生物性劳动的结果，而不是什么神启的“极简主义”。

A tomato sauce recipe, a narrative of "minimalism." Marcella Hazan’s fame rests on telling the world that no complex spices are needed—only butter, onion, and tomatoes. In the cultural layer, this is packaged as a pure aesthetic, tricking people into believing they've captured the essence of life through "primary ingredients." But this "simplicity" is a weaponized expression; it reduces cooking from a complex biochemical game to a mere a-to-b instruction set.

The most ironic detail is Step 3: "Discard the onion." The onion provides the foundational flavor and endures the most grueling chemical reactions, yet it is ordered to vanish before the final presentation. This is a microcosm of structural violence in the kitchen: certain elements are defined as "necessary support," yet they are not permitted to enter the space of the final "achievement." The onion disappears so the dish looks "lighter," conforming to a specific bourgeois aesthetic.

Those in the comments trying to "optimize" the recipe with pancetta or chicken are engaged in a game for an optimal expression. They seek subjectivity within a predefined rule (the recipe), yet they remain complicit in the logic that this authoritative "standard answer" is the benchmark. A just expression isn't about adhering to an authoritative recipe; it is about acknowledging that whether it's the richness of butter or the sacrifice of the onion, both are results of concrete biological labor, not some divine "minimalism."
