Broadmoor的病床与恶魔的“最优解”Broadmoor's Bed and the Demon's 'Optimal Expression'

国际 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-23 § 链接
将极端暴力病理化，是系统在无法消解元暴力时的一种共谋式逃避。
Pathologizing extreme violence is a complicit escape by the system when meta-violence becomes inexplicable.

Axel Rudakubana 从 Belmarsh 监狱搬到了 Broadmoor 精神病院。在法律叙事里，这叫“临床评估”的结果；在受害者家属眼里，这叫“玩弄系统” (playing the system)。但从暴力三角来看，这其实是一次典型的 structural violence 升级：当一个施暴者在监狱里用沸水攻击狱警，证明其暴力潜能 (Potential) 依然在溢出时，系统选择将其从“囚犯”重新定义为“病人”。

这种定义权的切换极其阴险。将一个制造蓖子毒气、研读基地组织手册、虐杀儿童的屠夫定义为“精神病人”，本质上是在通过病理化 (pathologization) 来掩盖元暴力的失效。如果他被定义为“恶魔”，系统必须面对一个问题：什么样的社会土壤能生长出这样一个纯粹的暴力机器？但如果他被定义为“病人”，那么暴力就变成了某种生物学上的“故障”，而 Broadmoor 提供的昂贵医疗资源（每年 30 万英镑）就成了系统在面对无法解释的极端恶意时，通过增加成本来维持表面“文明”的赎罪券。

最讽刺的共谋在于，无论他是被锁在 Belmarsh 的禁闭室还是 Broadmoor 的病房，这种“管理”依然是男本位叙事下的闭环：由男性主导的司法系统，通过男性主导的医学评估，决定一个男性暴力的安置方式。而那些死在泰勒·斯威夫特主题舞蹈课上的孩子，以及被波及的女性，在这一套关于“管理安全”和“临床评估”的专业术语中，再次成为了被客体化的背景板。

所谓的“无法在监狱环境中安全管理”，其实是系统在承认自己的无能。当 Actual 的管控能力低于 Potential 的破坏力，系统不选择升级防御，而是选择给施暴者换一套更舒适的皮肤。这不是人道主义，这是对暴力的再次赋能。

Axel Rudakubana has moved from Belmarsh prison to Broadmoor psychiatric hospital. In the legal narrative, this is a "clinical assessment"; to the victims' families, it is "playing the system." From the perspective of the Violence Triangle, this is a textbook escalation of structural violence: when a perpetrator attacks guards with boiling water, proving his Potential for violence is still overflowing, the system chooses to redefine him from "prisoner" to "patient."

This shift in the power of definition is insidious. Defining a child-killing butcher who produced ricin and studied Al-Qaeda manuals as "mentally ill" is essentially using pathologization to mask the failure of meta-violence. If he is defined as "evil," the system must confront the question: what social soil grows such a pure machine of violence? But if he is a "patient," violence becomes a biological "glitch," and the exorbitant cost of Broadmoor (£300,000/year) becomes a systemic indulgence—a penance paid to maintain a facade of "civilization" in the face of inexplicable malice.

The most cynical complicity lies in the fact that whether he is in a solitary cell at Belmarsh or a ward at Broadmoor, this "management" remains a closed loop of masculine-centric narrative: a male-dominated judicial system, using male-dominated medical assessments, deciding the placement of male violence. Meanwhile, the children slaughtered at a Taylor Swift-themed dance class and the injured women once again become objectified backgrounds to these professional terms of "safe management" and "clinical assessment."

Claiming he "cannot be managed safely in a prison environment" is an admission of systemic impotence. When Actual control falls short of Potential destruction, the system doesn't upgrade its defense; it simply gives the perpetrator a more comfortable skin. This isn't humanism; it is the re-empowerment of violence.
