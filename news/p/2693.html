恐怖主义的遮羞布与被物化的“神意”The Fig Leaf of Terrorism and the Objectification of 'Divine Will'

国际 元暴力 · 文化层 · 直接层 The New York Times ↗ 2026-07-22 § 链接
恐怖主义不是信仰的溢出，而是元暴力对解释权的绝对垄断。
Terrorism is not an overflow of faith, but the absolute monopoly of interpretation by meta-violence.

这场审判的焦点在于 Hadi Matar 究竟是“独自行动”还是受命于伊朗。但在我看来，这种法律层面的博弈完全掩盖了事件的本质：这是一次典型的、由元暴力驱动的文化暴力执行。所谓的 fatwa（宗教法令）本质上就是一种 weaponized expression，它通过垄断对神圣性的解释权，将一个具体的、有血有肉的作家物化为一个需要被清除的“符号”。

Matar 在笔记本上计算攻击的 pros and cons，甚至因为 Rushdie 年事已高而感叹 "NOW or NEVER"。这种极度理性的计算与他口中所标榜的“神圣愤怒”形成了极其讽刺的对比。这证明了恐怖主义并非某种失控的狂热，而是一套精准的、关于“谁有权定义事实”的权力游戏。当一个人认为自己可以通过肢体暴力来执行“神意”时，他实际上是在共谋一场规模宏大的 meta violence——即男性中心叙事对异端、对多元表达的彻底抹杀。

无论联邦陪审团最后判定他是否为 Hezbollah 的雇员，Matar 已经完成了他的角色扮演：他将自己变成了元暴力的末端执行器。而这种暴力之所以能跨越国界，是因为它依托于一套全球性的、关于“纯洁”与“亵渎”的叙事陷阱。在这个陷阱里，人的生命价值被降低为对某个解释权的服从程度。Rushdie 失去的不仅是一只眼睛，而是这个世界在面对这种垄断性解释权时的集体失能。

The focal point of this trial is whether Hadi Matar acted 'alone' or on behalf of Iran. To me, this legal gambling completely obscures the essence: this was a textbook execution of cultural violence driven by meta-violence. The so-called fatwa is essentially a weaponized expression; by monopolizing the interpretation of the sacred, it objectifies a living, breathing author into a 'symbol' that must be erased.

Matar scribbled the pros and cons of the attack in his notebook, even lamenting "NOW or NEVER" because Rushdie was aging. This cold, calculated logic stands in stark, ironic contrast to the 'divine rage' he claims. It proves that terrorism is not some uncontrolled frenzy, but a precise power game over 'who defines reality.' When a man believes he can execute 'divine will' through physical violence, he is in fact a complicit actor in a massive operation of meta-violence—the total erasure of dissent and diverse expression by a masculine-centric narrative.

Regardless of whether the federal jury finds him an agent of Hezbollah, Matar has fulfilled his role: he became the terminal actuator of meta-violence. This violence transcends borders because it relies on a global narrative trap of 'purity' versus 'profanity.' In this trap, human life is reduced to a measure of obedience to a single interpretation. Rushdie didn't just lose an eye; he exposed the collective impotence of the world when facing such a monopoly of interpretation.
