被吸出窗外的男人与Ryanair的PR谎言The Man Sucked Out the Window and Ryanair's PR Scam

其他 直接层 · 结构层 · 文化层 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-21 § 链接
直接暴力是可见的，而结构性暴力隐藏在PR术语的“现象级”掩盖之下。
Direct violence is visible; structural violence hides behind the 'phenomenal' mask of PR terminology.

一个61岁的男人在15,000英尺的高空被吸出机窗，在时速600公里的气流中半截身体在机外，这不仅是物理意义上的 direct violence，更是航空安全结构性失效的极端样本。在这种生死博弈中，真正起作用的不是所谓的“专业培训”，而是乘客之间自发的、非制度性的互救共谋——一个阿尔巴尼亚人用行李箱堵住窗户，才勉强将这个人的生命从死亡边缘拉回。

最令人作呕的是 Ryanair 的回应。公司将机组人员的表现定义为 “phenomenal job”（现象级的出色工作）。这是一个典型的 weaponized expression。当一个具体的受害者在经历面部变形、意识丧失和严重的躯体创伤时，公司通过一个宏大的、正面词汇的认知入口，试图将“恐惧导致的瘫痪”篡改为“专业的执行”。这种叙事操纵试图抹除一个事实：在真正的危机时刻，制度化的人员失效了，而幸存者依赖的是陌生人的善意。

Ryanair 的逻辑是：只要遵守了 safety protocols（安全协议），那么结果就是正确的。这正是 structural violence 的伪装——将“流程的合规”等同于“人的安全”。当受害者在要求补偿而非简单的“重新订票”邮件时，公司依然在用一套中立的、冷漠的商业语言来消解个体的痛苦。在这种男本位的、以效率和成本为核心的商业逻辑里，人的生命被简化为一张机票的重订成本。

A 61-year-old man being sucked out of an aircraft window at 15,000 feet, half his body exposed to 600km/h winds, is not just direct violence—it is an extreme specimen of structural failure in aviation safety. In this existential war between life and death, the only thing that actually worked was not 'professional training,' but a spontaneous, non-institutional complicity among passengers—an Albanian man using a suitcase to block the window, barely dragging a life back from the edge.

What is truly nauseating is Ryanair's response. The company described the crew's performance as a 'phenomenal job.' This is a textbook case of weaponized expression. While a concrete victim suffered facial deformation and loss of consciousness, the company used a grand, positive cognitive entry point to rewrite 'paralysis due to fear' as 'professional execution.' This narrative manipulation attempts to erase the fact that when the crisis hit, the institutionalized staff failed, and survival depended on the kindness of strangers.

Ryanair's logic is simple: if safety protocols were followed, the outcome is 'correct.' This is exactly how structural violence disguises itself—equating 'procedural compliance' with 'human safety.' While the victim asks for compensation instead of mere 'rebooking emails,' the company continues to use a neutral, cold commercial language to dissolve individual suffering. In this masculine-centric business logic, where efficiency and cost are the only metrics, a human life is reduced to the cost of a rebooked ticket.
