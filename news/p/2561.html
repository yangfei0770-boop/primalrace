用“熟悉”来掩盖结构性冷漠的 scamThe Scam of Using 'Familiarity' to Mask Structural Indifference

哲学 结构层 · 文化层 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-20 § 链接
当企业用“习惯性悲剧”来正当化拒绝共情时，它在执行一种结构暴力。
When a company justifies a lack of empathy by claiming 'familiarity with tragedy,' it is exercising structural violence.

这家店的回复简直是教科书级别的 cultural violence。它说自己“遗憾地熟悉”这类情况，试图用一种伪装成经验的冷漠，将具体的个体之死转化为一个标准化的“业务场景”。这是一种极其恶劣的 weaponized 叙事：通过宣称“我们见多了”，从而把拒绝退款的行为包装成一种“专业且一致”的原则，掩盖其本质上是对弱势者剩余价值的最后一次榨取。

在这个博弈中，店方握有绝对的定价权和解释权。他们在 T&C（条款与条件）里写着“酌情处理”，这意味着 compassion（共情）在他们的系统里不是一种道德自觉，而是一个可以被随意开关的权力工具。当一个母亲请求退回一个从未离开过后备箱、且代表着死亡提醒的轮椅时，店方选择关闭这个开关。这种对 discretion（裁量权）的垄断，就是典型的 structural violence——制度设计之初就确保了资本在面对人类最深沉的痛苦时，依然能维持其利润的绝对纯净。

最讽刺的是，这种冷漠被冠以“不以盈利为乐”的虚伪面具。事实上，他们不仅在盈利，更在通过这种“原则性”的拒绝，完成对弱势者主体性的最后一次抹除。对于那个死去的 46 岁单亲母亲及其后代来说，这 435 英镑是实际的生存资源，而对于这家店来说，这只是一个无需执行的退款指令。Potential（本可达到的体面）与 Actual（冰冷的现实）之间的差额，就是这家店在用制度之名施行的暴力。

The store's response is a textbook example of cultural violence. By claiming to be 'regrettably familiar' with such circumstances, they attempt to transform a specific, visceral human loss into a standardized 'business scenario.' This is a weaponized narrative: by asserting that they have 'seen it all,' they package the refusal of a refund as a 'professional and consistent' principle, masking the fact that they are simply extracting the last bit of value from a vulnerable party.

In this game, the store holds absolute pricing and interpretive power. Their T&Cs state that goodwill is 'at the discretion of the company,' meaning compassion in their system is not a moral impulse but a power tool that can be toggled on or off. When a mother begs to return a wheelchair that never left the boot—a physical reminder of death—the store chooses to switch that toggle off. This monopoly over discretion is the essence of structural violence: the system is designed to ensure that capital remains pristine, even when faced with the most profound human suffering.

The most cynical part is the hypocritical mask of 'taking no pleasure' in the process. In reality, they are not just profiting; they are erasing the subjecthood of the marginalized through this 'principled' refusal. For the deceased 46-year-old single mother and her teenage daughter, those £435 are actual survival resources; for the store, it is merely a refund order that doesn't need to be executed. The gap between the Potential (a decent, empathetic outcome) and the Actual (cold indifference) is the violence this store executes in the name of policy.
