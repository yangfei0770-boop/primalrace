被子弹击中的脚与被掩盖的共谋The Shot Foot and the Masked Complicity

国际 直接层 · 结构层 · 文化层 The New York Times ↗ 2026-07-24 § 链接
直接暴力只是结构暴力的尖端，而共谋者总在开枪后迅速切割。
Direct violence is just the tip of structural violence; complicitors always cut ties after the shot.

这起枪击案不是一个简单的“情绪失控”个案，而是一次典型的 Violence Triangle 联动。Brandon Booth 开枪的行为是 direct violence，但其底色是 structural violence：GEO Group 这种私有化监狱运营商将剥削和禁锢商品化，把移民拘留中心变成一个巨大的权力黑盒。当抗议者试图通过表达来撕开这个黑盒时，这种结构性的压迫在 Booth 身上具象化为了攻击性的暴力。

值得玩味的是警察的叙事——他们使用了“initiated a verbal confrontation”（发起言语冲突）和“impeding”（阻碍）这样的词汇。这是一种典型的 cultural violence，试图通过定义“谁先挑衅”来稀释施暴者的正当性，将一个持枪射击大学生的行为，包装成一次由于“被阻碍”而引发的冲突。在这种叙事中，抗议者的主体性被简化为“麻烦制造者”，而施暴者的暴力被暗示为一种对“秩序”受损的过度反应。

至于 GEO Group 迅速地将 Booth “terminated”（解雇），这不过是共谋者在面对公关危机时的标准操作。公司在享受私有化监狱带来的利润（complicity）时，绝不会在员工用私枪射击平民这种低级错误导致法律风险时提供庇护。切割不是因为正义，而是因为这种暴力不再符合其“专业管理”的 PR 形象。这种快速的切割掩盖了最核心的问题：在这样一个权力极度不对等的场域里，暴力是如何被默许甚至鼓励的？

Emma Landis 拄着拐杖决定返回抗议，这是在存在性战争中一次强有力的表达。当 Actual（被击中的脚）与 Potential（自由表达权利）之间产生巨大的暴力差额时，她选择用身体重新夺回解释权。但我们要警惕，如果司法审判仅仅停留在对 Booth 个人行为的惩罚，而没有追究 GEO Group 这种私有化禁锢机制的结构性罪恶，那么这次“正义”仅仅是一次表演性的让步。

This shooting is not a simple case of "emotional instability," but a textbook linkage of the Violence Triangle. Brandon Booth's act is direct violence, yet its foundation is structural violence: the GEO Group, as a private prison operator, commodifies exploitation and confinement, turning detention centers into massive black boxes of power. When protesters use expression to tear open this box, the structural oppression manifests in Booth as aggressive violence.

Notably, the police narrative employs terms like "initiated a verbal confrontation" and "impeding." This is classic cultural violence, attempting to dilute the perpetrator's culpability by defining "who provoked whom," framing the act of shooting a college student as a reaction to "disrupted order." In this narrative, the protesters' agency is reduced to being "troublemakers," while the violence is hinted to be an overreaction to a loss of "order."

As for GEO Group's swift decision to "terminate" Booth, it is a standard operation for complicitors facing a PR crisis. A company profiting from the complicity of privatized incarceration will never protect an employee when a clumsy error—like using a personal pistol on a civilian—creates legal liability. The cut is not about justice, but because this violence no longer fits their PR image of "professional management." This rapid dissociation masks the core issue: how is violence tacitly permitted or even encouraged in a field of such extreme power imbalance?

Emma Landis's decision to return to protests on crutches is a powerful expression in the existential war. When a massive gap of violence opens between the Actual (a shot foot) and the Potential (the right to free expression), she chooses to reclaim the power of interpretation with her body. However, we must remain vigilant: if the judicial outcome merely punishes Booth's individual act without addressing the structural evil of the GEO Group's privatization mechanism, this "justice" remains nothing more than a performative concession.
