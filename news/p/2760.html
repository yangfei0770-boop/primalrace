法律的“文明”掩体与恐惧的计算The 'Civilized' Mask of Law and the Calculus of Fear

哲学 结构层 · 文化层 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-23 § 链接
当辩护成为一种风险，正义就成了权力的PR表演。
When defense becomes a risk, justice is reduced to a PR performance for power.

这起关于 Rajiv Menon 的藐视法庭案，精准地揭示了结构性暴力 (structural violence) 是如何通过一个名为“程序正义”的文明掩体来实施的。在法律的叙事中，法官 Johnson 维护的是“法庭秩序”，但在实际的博弈中，他通过威胁律师的职业生存，在刑事司法系统中制造了一次极其高效的 chilling effect。

这就是典型的表达武器化：权力中心通过定义什么是“可接受的言论”，夺取了对事实解释权的垄断。当一名律师因为讨论加沙战争或质疑制度性不端而面临两年监禁时，法律不再是保护公民的盾牌，而成了国家机器用来筛选“顺从者”的筛子。这种威胁迫使所有辩护律师在“最优解表达”之间进行一种绝望的计算：是追求 100% 的公正表达来挑战权力，还是通过自我审查来换取职业安全？

在这种博弈中，主体性被悄悄杀死了。当律师开始 self-censor，他们就成了这个暴力结构的共谋者 (complicit)。这种共谋不需要显性的协议，只需要一种对“风险”的共同认知。最终，法庭上的对抗演变成了一场表演，而真正的正义——即 Potential 与 Actual 之间那个被暴力填满的差额——被永久地掩盖在了法官的 approved judgment 之下。

The contempt case against Rajiv Menon precisely reveals how structural violence operates through a civilized mask called 'procedural justice.' In the legal narrative, Justice Johnson is maintaining 'court order,' but in the actual game, he creates a highly efficient chilling effect by threatening a barrister's professional survival.

This is a textbook example of the weaponization of expression: the center of power seizes the monopoly over the interpretation of facts by defining what constitutes 'acceptable speech.' When a lawyer faces two years in prison for discussing the war in Gaza or challenging institutional misconduct, the law ceases to be a shield for citizens and becomes a sieve used by the state machinery to filter for 'the compliant.' This threat forces all defense advocates into a desperate calculation between a Just Expression that challenges power and a fake optimal expression that ensures professional safety.

In this game, subjectivity is quietly murdered. When advocates begin to self-censor, they become complicit in this violent structure. This complicity requires no explicit agreement, only a shared recognition of 'risk.' Ultimately, the courtroom confrontation evolves into a performance, and true justice—the gap between Potential and Actual filled by violence—is permanently buried beneath the judge's approved judgment.
