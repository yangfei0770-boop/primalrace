“主人”的幻觉与被武器化的圣地The Illusion of 'Ownership' and the Weaponized Sanctuary

国际 直接层 · 结构层 · 文化层 · 元暴力 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-23 § 链接
宗教叙事是掩体，权力对解释权的垄断才是真正的元暴力。
Religious narratives are mere covers; the monopoly over the power of interpretation is the true meta-violence.

Ben-Gvir 说犹太人在那里“感觉像主人” (feel like the owners)，这句话精准地撕开了宗教外衣，露出了最底层的权力逻辑。所谓的“圣地之争”从来不是关于神，而是关于谁拥有定义“事实”的权力，以及谁能将这种定义权转化为物理上的支配。当国家安全部长带着武装警察冲进圣地，这已经不是宗教仪式，而是一次典型的表达武器化：通过在对方的认知入口处强行植入“主权”符号（国旗、国歌），完成一次对空间的暴力标记。

这套操作是典型的元暴力 (meta violence) 逻辑。它通过重新定义“现状” (status quo)，将原本的协议撕毁，然后宣布这种破坏就是“进步”。在这种叙事中，宗教成了最方便的武器化入口——利用 Tisha B’Av 的历史伤痕来合法化当下的侵略。当 Netanyahu 在一方面撕毁协议，另一方面又坚称没有违规时，他是在通过操纵语言来制造一个虚假的现实。这种对解释权的垄断，正是所有结构性暴力能够持续运作的底层代码。

最令人作呕的共谋在于，这种“主人”意识不仅在政治高层被推演，更在极端主义者中被内化。他们通过扮演“圣地收复者”这个角色，在存在性战争中寻求一种廉价的、通过凌辱他者而获得的自我确证。当武装警察为这些极端分子开路，国家机器就变成了共谋者，将直接暴力 (direct violence) 包装成宗教虔诚。这种从文化层到结构层再到直接层的联动，将整个地区推向一个 Potential 与 Actual 差额巨大的暴力深渊。

Ben-Gvir’s claim that Jews there “feel like the owners” precisely strips away the religious facade to reveal the underlying power logic. The so-called "struggle for holy sites" has never been about God; it is about who controls the power to define "reality" and how to translate that definition into physical dominance. When a National Security Minister storms a sanctuary escorted by armed police, it is no longer a religious rite, but a textbook case of the weaponisation of expression: forcibly implanting symbols of sovereignty (flags, anthems) at the other's cognitive entry point to achieve a violent marking of space.

This operation follows the logic of meta-violence. By redefining the "status quo," they tear up existing agreements and then announce that this destruction constitutes "progress." In this narrative, religion serves as the most convenient entry point for weaponisation—utilizing the historical trauma of Tisha B’Av to legitimize current aggression. When Netanyahu tears up the agreement while insisting no violation occurred, he is manipulating language to manufacture a fake reality. This monopoly over interpretation is the underlying code that allows all structural violence to persist.

The most sickening part is the complicity. This "owner" consciousness is not only performed by the political elite but is internalized by extremists. By playing the role of "sanctuary reclaimers," they seek a cheap confirmation of existence achieved through the humiliation of others. When armed police clear the path for these extremists, the state apparatus becomes a co-conspirator, packaging direct violence as religious piety. This linkage—from the cultural layer to the structural and finally the direct layer—pushes the entire region toward a violent abyss where the gap between Potential and Actual becomes catastrophic.
