FIFA的“中立”不过是权力的润滑剂FIFA's 'Neutrality' is Just a Lubricant for Power

国际 结构层 · 文化层 The New York Times ↗ 2026-07-20 § 链接
中立是弱者的幻想，而FIFA的中立是权力的定价权。
Neutrality is a fantasy for the weak; for FIFA, it is the pricing power of authority.

FIFA一直试图扮演一个超脱于政治之上的“体育圣殿”，但这次2026年世界杯把这层遮羞布撕得体无完肤。所谓的 neutrality（中立）在 Trump 的一个电话面前，脆弱得像一张纸。当一个国家的最高权力者可以直接干预规则来为本国争取利益时，FIFA 就不再是一个非营利组织，而是一个典型的共谋场域。

这场赛事是典型的 structural violence：通过将球队扩充到 48 支，FIFA 并不是为了所谓的“足球普及”，而是在制造更多的商业入口。130 亿美元的预期利润，是通过抬高票价将底层球迷 priced out（排除在外）换来的。这种商业逻辑极其 masculine-centric：追求规模的无限扩张，追求利润的最大化，而将体育的纯粹性客体化为一种可交易的商品。

最讽刺的是，这种权力的博弈被包装成“史上最伟大的体育盛事”。当 Trump 与 Infantino 并肩站在领奖台上时，他们完成了一次极其高效的共谋——权力者通过操纵规则获得政治资本，而组织者通过出卖原则获得商业暴利。在这种叙事中，足球不再是关于竞技，而成了权力者进行存在性战争的背景板。

所谓的“改变足球”，其实是把足球彻底变成了权力的附庸。当规则可以被电话通话所修改，足球的公正表达就彻底死亡了，剩下的只有对权力的崇拜和对金钱的贪婪。

FIFA has long performed the role of a 'sporting sanctuary' transcending politics, but the 2026 World Cup has ripped this veil away. Their claim to neutrality is as fragile as a piece of paper when faced with a single phone call from Trump. When the highest power of a nation can directly manipulate rules to favor its own, FIFA ceases to be a nonprofit and becomes a textbook site of complicity.

This tournament is a manifestation of structural violence. Expanding to 48 teams wasn't about 'globalizing soccer,' but about manufacturing more commercial entry points. The projected $13 billion profit was built by pricing out the working-class fans. This logic is aggressively masculine-centric: prioritizing infinite expansion and maximum profit while objectifying the purity of sport into a tradable commodity.

The ultimate irony is that this power game is packaged as 'one of the greatest sporting events in history.' As Trump and Infantino stood together on the podium, they completed a highly efficient act of complicity—the power-player gains political capital by manipulating rules, while the organizer gains commercial windfalls by selling out principles. In this narrative, soccer is no longer about the game; it is merely a backdrop for the existential war of the powerful.

To say this 'changed soccer forever' is to admit that soccer has been fully subsumed by power. When rules can be rewritten via a phone call, any Just Expression in the sport dies, leaving behind only the worship of power and the greed for gold.
