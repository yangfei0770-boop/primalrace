黄金股：一场关于“控制权”的PR骗局The Golden Share: A PR Scam of 'Control'

国际 结构层 · 文化层 The Guardian ↗ 2026-07-21 § 链接
用象征性的权力让渡掩盖结构性的债务转移，是典型的资本共谋。
Using symbolic power concessions to mask structural debt transfer is a classic act of capital complicity.

所谓的“黄金股” (golden share) 根本不是什么公共控制的升级，而是一次极其精巧的 weaponized expression。投资者 L&VW 试图用一个“否决权”的虚名，去交换一个能继续持有 170 亿英镑债务、且无需在短期内面对国家化清算的生存空间。这在博弈论中叫作“假.最优解表达”：通过扮演一个“愿意配合公共利益”的角色，来掩盖其本质上是对公共资源进行掠夺后的逃逸。

这场戏最恶劣的地方在于它的共谋机制 (complicity)。投资者承诺 10 年不拿分红、扩大社会资费，这些听起来很 nice 的条款，本质上是文化暴力 (cultural violence) 的掩体。他们试图在叙事上将“救赎方案”与“国家化”对立，暗示国家化会给纳税人带来 20 亿英镑的账单，从而诱导政府在恐惧中接受这个名为“共谋”的协议。这种逻辑是：只要我给了你一个能决定“不做什么”的按钮（黄金股），你就可以在法律上承认我继续拥有这个基础设施的实际收益权。

从加尔通的暴力三角来看，这依然是 structural violence 的延续。Thames Water 的债务危机是私有化逻辑下的必然结果——将公共基础设施的 Potential（长期稳定运行）转化为资本的 Actual（短期高额分红），差额就是暴力。现在，这群资本共谋者试图通过一次“表演性让步”，让政府成为他们债务游戏的背书人。如果 Andy Burnham 接受了这套叙事，那么这次“救赎”将成为元暴力 (meta violence) 的又一次胜利：权力依然在资本手中，而责任被巧妙地分摊给了公众。

The so-called “golden share” is not an upgrade in public control, but a sophisticated piece of weaponized expression. The L&VW consortium is attempting to trade a nominal “veto power” for the survival of their £17bn debt position, avoiding the immediate reckoning of nationalisation. In game theory, this is a “fake optimal expression”: playing the role of a “public-interest partner” to mask an escape attempt after looting public resources.

The most sinister part of this play is the mechanism of complicity. Promises of no dividends for 10 years and expanded social tariffs are merely cultural violence acting as a shield. They are attempting to frame the narrative as a binary choice between their “rescue plan” and “nationalisation,” suggesting the latter imposes a £2bn bill on taxpayers. This is a psychological trap designed to make the government accept a complicit agreement out of fear. The logic is simple: as long as I give you a button to say “no” (the golden share), you concede my actual right to the infrastructure's returns.

Applying Galtung’s Violence Triangle, this remains a continuation of structural violence. The Thames Water crisis is the inevitable result of privatization logic—converting the Potential of public infrastructure into the Actual of short-term dividends; the gap is the violence. Now, these co-conspirators are using a “performative concession” to make the government an underwriter for their debt game. If Andy Burnham buys into this narrative, it will be another victory for meta-violence: power remains with capital, while the liability is subtly shifted to the public.
